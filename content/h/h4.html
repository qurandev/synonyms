Yadu’u, Hatana (to repel)

Pg. 881

Pdf pg. 899

1. Dafa’aa: To protect from external danger or to repel an attack.

Daf’aae: To protect something.

Muda’fat: To attack in defense.

Allah SWT said (22:40): “And if Allah SWT hadn’t stopped people from attacking each other.”

If this word ends with “ilaa”, it means to give somebody their trust/possession back.

In Quran (4:6): “So, return their possessions.”

2. Janaba/Janna’ba: To repel, to save someone/something by pulling them on the side. To tap on the side of the body.

Janib: Side of a body

Allah SWT said: Wasayujanabuha al’ataqa al’adhi yuti malahu yatazakah “The one who abstains from sins will be pulled aside—the one who gives in charity.”

3. Zahzaha: To repel someone away from some place, resign

Al-zahzaha: Far

Zahzaha-An: To repel someone away from harm

Allah SWT said (3:185): “And the one, who is pulled away/repelled away from (hell) and was entered into paradise, is saved.”

4. Da’raa: to yank away someone with extreme force and to save him/her from pain or injury.

Allah SWT says (24:8): “and if this woman takes oath by Allah SWT four times then this will save her from punishment.”

Idraa’: To blame someone else for one’s own misdeeds to protect oneself from punishment.

Allah SWT says (2:72):“And you start blaming one another for murdering that person.”

5. Da’aa: To shove away, jostle away, elbow roughly and rudely.

Allah SWT says (107:2): “A man like this repulses/kicks away/jostles away an orphan!”

6. Dahra: 1) Repel someone with disgust, 2) To push away in loathing, abhor someone

Allah SWT said (7:18): “Allah SWT told (Ibless/Satan) to get out of the paradise.”

7. Khasaa/Khasaa nazar: weak eyesight

Khasa alqalub: to repulse a dog

Khaseeye minalqab wakhanazeer: Disgustingly repulse a panting dog or pig that is not allowed to wander among people.

Allah SWT says (2:65): “And we told them to become disgraced monkeys.”

Summary:

1. Dafa’aa: To repel away the danger/attack to protect oneself

2. Janaba: To pull someone aside (common usage)

3. Zahzaha: To pull away someone from a place and to save them from injury

4. Da’raa: To keep away the harm/injury with full might and extreme force

5. Da’aa: To jostle someone around or to kick someone out insultingly

6. Dahra: To kick someone out by humiliating them. No matter whatever the reason

7. Khasaa: To assume someone, something is disgusting/ filthy and to abhor them
