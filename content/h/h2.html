2 HAAN [yes]

Synonyms: NA’AM, AYEE, BALAA

2.1 NA’AM (نَعَمْ): Means yes. A word for affirmation. An answer to a question where both the question and answer is positive. In the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:113

وَجَاءَ السَّحَرَةُ فِرْعَوْنَ قَالُوا إِنَّ لَنَا لَأَجْرًا إِن كُنَّا نَحْنُ الْغَالِبِينَ قَالَ نَعَمْ وَإِنَّكُمْ لَمِنَ الْمُقَرَّبِينَ  

They said: "Indeed there will be a (good) reward for us if we are the victors." He said: "Yes, and moreover you will (in that case) be of the nearest (to me)."

 

2.2 AYEE (اِی): Means ‘yes yes’; certainly. Word for affirmation. Used to give a positive answer with confirmation and emphasis in case of a question, so that there is no room left for doubt or suspicion. Usually is followed by swearing or vow. Allah says in the Quran

Surat Yunus (Jonah) - سورة يونس 10:53

وَيَسْتَنبِئُونَكَ أَحَقٌّ هُوَ ۖ قُلْ إِي وَرَبِّي إِنَّهُ لَحَقٌّ

And they ask information of you, [O Muhammad], "Is it true?" Say, "Yes, by my Lord. Indeed, it is truth; and you will not cause failure [to Allah ]."

 

2.3 BALAA (بَلَىٰ ): Is also a word for affirmation, but is used when the question is giving a negative gesture. And in case of answer one has to change this negativity to positivity. And it means ‘why not - certainly’.

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:172

بِرَبِّكُمْ ۖ قَالُوا بَلَىٰ

"Am I not your Lord?" They said, "Yes indeed,
