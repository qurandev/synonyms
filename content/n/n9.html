9 NAAM – NAAM RAKHNA [name, to name]

Synonyms: ISM, SAMMA, LAQAB, NABAZA, FULAN

Page 841

 

9.1 ISM (اِسْم): Means a name of something, SAMW (سمو) means loftiness. A noun is called ISM (اسم) because it is used to introduce something and it comes under discussion (Plural ISMAA (اسمإ)). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:31

وَعَلَّمَ آدَمَ الْأَسْمَاءَ كُلَّهَا

And He taught Adam the names, all of them;

 And SAMMA (سَمَّی) means to name someone or something. And while naming, taking in account the qualities of that person or thing is not necessary. In the Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:71

أَتُجَادِلُونَنِي فِي أَسْمَاءٍ سَمَّيْتُمُوهَا أَنتُمْ وَآبَاؤُكُم

Are you arguing with me over names, which you and your ancestors invented, 

And SAMIYAN (سَمِیًّا) means a namesake. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Maryam (Mary): سُوۡرَةُ مَریَم 19:7

اسْمُهُ يَحْيَىٰ لَمْ نَجْعَل لَّهُ مِن قَبْلُ سَمِيًّا

His name will be Yahya (John). We have given that name to none before (him)."

9.2 LAQAB (لقب): Apart from real name, this sort of name takes in account  the qualities of the named person. For instance MASEEH is the title of HAZRAT EESA (A.S) (Jesus) who spent his whole life in journey and travelling and never settled down into a married life. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ale- Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:45

إِذْ قَالَتِ الْمَلَائِكَةُ يَا مَرْيَمُ إِنَّ اللَّهَ يُبَشِّرُكِ بِكَلِمَةٍ مِّنْهُ اسْمُهُ الْمَسِيحُ عِيسَى ابْنُ مَرْيَمَ

The Angels said, “O Mary, God gives you good news of a Word from Him. His name is the Messiah, Jesus, son of Mary, 

In this Ayah, MASEEH (مسيح) is a LAQAB (لقب) and IBN-MARYAM (ابن مريم) (Son of Mary) is the lineage, which Allah has used with name of mother because he had no father.

9.3 NABAZA (نَبَزَ): AN-NABZ (اَلنَّبْز) is also a type of LAQAB (لقب) (title), but it is used to represent the bad qualities of a person regardless of a person having them or not and these qualities can be related to bloodline or morals. NABAZA (نَبَزَ) means to name someone in offensive words or name calling (dictionary al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hujraat (The Inner Apartments): سُوۡرَةُ الحُجرَات 49:11

وَلَا تَنَابَزُوا بِالْأَلْقَابِ

 nor shall you insult one another with names.

9.4 FULAN (فُلَانٌ): (Feminine FULANATUN (فُلَانَةٌ)) is a replacement to ISM (اسم). When used for all the intelligent things then this word can be preceded by  ‘AL’ (ال). For instance RAKABTU-FULANA‘رَکَبْتُ فُلَانَ’ means he rode so and so animal (male) and HALABTU-FULANATA ‘حَلَبْتُ فُلَانَةَ’ means he milked so and so animal (female). In the Quran

Surah Al-Furqaan (The criterion): سورۃ الفُرْقان 25:28

يَا وَيْلَتَىٰ لَيْتَنِي لَمْ أَتَّخِذْ فُلَانًا خَلِيلًا

Oh, woe to me; I wish I never took so-and-so for a friend.

Summary:

9.1 ISM: A name that is used to introduce anything.

9.2 LAQAB: Name based on a quality.

9.3 NABAZA: To call names.

9.4 FULAN: Instead of naming something call it ‘so and so’ or ‘such a one’.
