17 NASEEHAT, NASEEHAT KERNA, HASIL KERNA

[Advice, to advise, to take advice]

Synonyms: NASAHA(نصح), ZAKKAR(ضد), WA’Z(وعظ), WASSA(وصى), I’BRAH(عبرة), I’TABAR(اعتبر)

17.1 nasaha(نصح): to think good about someone. Naseeha(نصيحة) is all those sayings and acts which includes well wishing for others. Allah states that:    

________________

Surat Hud  - سُوۡرَةُ هُود

11:34

وَلَا يَنفَعُكُمۡ نُصۡحِىٓ إِنۡ أَرَدتُّ أَنۡ أَنصَحَ لَكُمۡ إِن كَانَ ٱللَّهُ يُرِيدُ أَن يُغۡوِيَكُمۡ‌ۚ

My counsel will not profit you if I were minded to advise you, if Allah's will is to keep you astray. He is your Lord and unto Him ye will be brought back.

________________

17.2 zakkara (ذكر): zakara(ذكر) meaning to remember and miss someone (ant: nasiya(نسي)) and zikr(ذكر) means to bring something in heart or on tongue. Tazkara(تذكره) means all those things through which you remember some of your needs, certificate, passport, tickets etc. The Holy Quran is also called zikr(ذكر) and tazkara(تذكره) because it also reminds one of the natural preaching and holding of witness for goodness in humans. Zikr(ذكر) also means all those advices which make you remember Allah and whatever reminds you of Allah, is zikr(ذكر). Zakkara(ذكر) means to advise and bring to attention. Zakkar-al-qaum(ذكرالقوم) means that he gave advice to his nation. Allah states that:

Surat An-Nisa (Women) - سُوۡرَةُ النِّسَاء

4:12

مِنۢ بَعۡدِ وَصِيَّةٍ۬ يُوصِينَ بِهَآ أَوۡ دَيۡنٍ۬‌ۚ

17.5 I’bra and I’tabar(عبرة, اعتبر): a’boor(عبور) is basically used to depict how the water is crossed whether it is through swimming or boat, animal or through a bridge. And a’br-un-nahr(عبرالنهر) is that place where you go inside the water and cross it. Similarly, a’br-ul-ain(عبرالعين) means the flowing of tears through the eyes and a’bra(عبر) meaning tears and a’bara(عبر) means to be sad, to cry. A’br-al-ain(عبرالعين) means filling of eyes with tears. A’brata(عبرة) means to receive advice on some matter through consideration and pondering. It is said

لك بفلان عبرة Which means to consider the situation of the person to find out about the actual cause of the event. Allah states that:

Surat Al-Hashr (Exile) - سُوۡرَةُ الحَشر

59: 2

وَقَذَفَ فِى قُلُوبِہِمُ ٱلرُّعۡبَ‌ۚ يُخۡرِبُونَ بُيُوتَہُم بِأَيۡدِيہِمۡ وَأَيۡدِى ٱلۡمُؤۡمِنِينَ فَٱعۡتَبِرُواْ يَـٰٓأُوْلِى ٱلۡأَبۡصَـٰرِ

He it is Who hath caused those of the People of the Scripture who disbelieved to go forth from their homes unto the first exile. Ye deemed not that they would go forth, while they deemed that their strongholds would protect them from Allah. But Allah reached them from a place whereof they recked not, and cast terror in their hearts so that they ruined their houses with their own hands and the hands of the believers. So learn a lesson, O ye who have eyes!

Summary:

1. NASAHA(نصح): to talk or act as well wishing for someone.

2. ZAKKARA(ذكر): that advice which reminds you of God

3.  WA’ZA(وعظ): to advise through intimidation and persuasion.

4. WASSA(وصى): to instruct someone and give advice before an event has taken place.

5. I’TABAR(اعتبر): to think about the causes of a distressing event and take advice from it .
