La - Not

Various words have been used in the Quran for this.

1- Laesa

It is used in a similar way as “not” is used in English.

2- La, Lum, Lun

'La', whenever used with a verb makes it negative as in 'La Yaddrib', translated as 'he does not hit' or 'he will not hit'. If, however, 'Lum' is used, it depicts past tense as well as making it negative, for example, 'Lum yaddrib' means 'he did not hit'. 'Lun', however, changes it to future and is furthur used to strengthen the negation, for example, 'Lun yaddriba' means, 'he (definitely) won't hit'.

3- Inna, Maa, Hul

These are 3 words that have different meanings of their own but if 'Illa' follows any of these, it changes their meanings to 'no' or 'not'. For example, 'In antum illa takzibun' translated as, 'You are not but liars', meaning you like to tell lies. Similarly, 'Ma min ilaahin il Allah' meaning, there is no diety except Allah. 'Hal kuntu illa basharan rasoolan' meaning, I am not but a messenger.

For 'no' or 'not', the following are regularly used, La, Lamma, Bala, Kalla, Innama, Ma and Laat.

a- La

La is used frequently to change an affirmative statement to negative and to joing statements together.

“And the disbeliever had not believed, nor had he prayed” 75/31

b- Kama

Kama is commonly used to negate an incident in the past. It carries the meaning of 'yet' in English.

“Do you think that you will be left [as you are] while Allah has not yet made evident those among you who strive” 9/16

c- Bala

When the question is in negative and the answer is in affirmative but it rectifies the negation in the question, then Bala is used as in 'why not'.

“Am I not your Lord?" They said, "Yes, why not [we have testified]” 7/172

d- Kalla

It means never or something that is ever impossible. It is used to serve caution and to negate the last statement.

“About what are they asking one another? About the great news - That over which they are in disagreement. No! They are going to know. Then, no! They are going to know” 78/1-5

e- Inni

Inni means 'other than', for example

“Say, "I am only a man like you [other than], to whom has been revealed ” 18/110

f- Ma

It is used in various ways. When there is a claim in a statement, it negates it and so is called Ma of negation.

“Perfect is Allah ! This is not a man; this is none but a noble angel ” 12/31

g- Lat

It is used in the same meaning as Laesa and is of Yemini origin.

“And they [then] called out; but it was not a time for escape” 38/3

It means that they started calling out for forgiveness when it was not the time for forgiveness.
