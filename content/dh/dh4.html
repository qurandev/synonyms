4- ZALEEL [Inferior]

 

Qur’an has referred them by the words AZILLA (أذلة ) MAHEEN (مهين ) SAGHIRUN (صاغر ) DAKHIRUN (دآخر ) ARAZILLUN (أراذل ) ASFALLO (اسفل ) KHASI (خاسی )

 

 

4.1   AZILLA  (ازلة ): Plural of ZALEEL (ذليل ) its antonym is IZAA (عزة ) which is the plural of AZEEZ (عزيز ) here we generally refer ZALEEL (ذليل ) as stingy or mean and we refer AZEEZ (عزيز ) as close relative. Both of these meanings are wrong by dictionary. In reality, ZALEEL (ذليل ) means one who is inferior and AZEEZ (عزيز ) means one who is superior. The concept of  ZALEEL (ذليل ) is literally incomplete without comparing it with the concept of AZEEZ (عزيز ) Qur’an has also used this word in same meaning:

Surat 'Āli `Imrān (Family of Imran) - سورة آل عمران 3:123

  وَلَقَدْ نَصَرَكُمُ اللّٰهُ بِبَدْرٍ وَّاَنْتُمْ اَذِلَّةٌ

And already had Allah given you victory at [the battle of] Badr while you were few in number.

4.2   MAHEEN  (مهين ): means been inferior, been weak and MAHEEN (مهين ) means small, not valued, disgraced and friendless. And according to Imam Raghib, MAHEEN (مهين )  is a person on which someone else is imposed and make him disgraced. It is in Qur’an

Surat Az-Zukhruf (The Ornaments of Gold) - سورة الزخرف 43:52

   اَمْ اَنَا خَيْرٌ مِّنْ ھٰذَا الَّذِيْ هُوَ مَهِيْنٌ

Or am I [not] better than this one who is insignificant?

4.3   SAGHIR  ( صاغر ): (it is opposite of KIBR (كبر ) )and SAGHAR (صغار ) means disgrace and SAGHIR SAGHIRUN (صاغر ) means someone with secondary status and the one who is forced to follow orders with derangement of superiority and the one who accept and acknowledge this. It is in Qur’an:

Surat At-Tawbah (The Repentance) - سورة التوبة 9:29

   حَتّٰي يُعْطُوا الْجِزْيَةَ عَنْ يَّدٍ وَّهُمْ صٰغِرُوْنَ

 [fight] until they give the jizyah willingly while they are humbled.

 

4.4   DAKHIR  (داخر ): DAKHARA (دخر) has three meanings i.e. Humbleness, Humiliation and Disrespectfulness. And DAKHIRA (دخر) means the one who accepts the humiliated obedience due to the lack of wisdom and understanding. It is in Qur’an

 

(Surat-Momin) (True Believer) -  سورة مومن 40:60

        اِنَّ الَّذِيْنَ يَسْتَكْبِرُوْنَ عَنْ عِبَادَتِيْ سَيَدْخُلُوْنَ جَهَنَّمَ دٰخِرِيْنَ            

  Indeed, those who disdain My worship will enter Hell [rendered] contemptible.

 At another occasion it is said:

 

Surat An-Naml (The Ant) - سورة النمل 27:87

  وَ يَوْمَ يُنْفَخُ فِي الصُّوْرِ فَفَزِعَ مَنْ فِي السَّمٰوٰتِ وَمَنْ فِي الْاَرْضِ اِلَّا مَنْ شَاۗءَ اللّٰهُ  ۭ وَكُلٌّ اَتَوْهُ دٰخِرِيْنَ

And [warn of] the Day the Horn will be blown, and whoever is in the heavens and whoever is on the earth will be terrified except whom Allah wills. And all will come to Him humbled.

 

4.5   ARAZAL  (ارآزل ): Plural of ARZAL (ارذل )RAZAL (أراذل ) means able to be disrespectful, evil. And ARAZAL (أراذل ) means crap, faulty and trash. Those low class people to whom other people do not get closer due to their low class.  It is in Qur’an

 

Surat Hūd (Hud) - سورة هود 11:27

 وَمَا نَرٰىكَ اتَّبَعَكَ اِلَّا الَّذِيْنَ هُمْ اَرَاذِلُـنَا بَادِيَ الرَّاْيِ           

 And we do not see you followed except by those who are the lowest of us [and] at first suggestion.

 

4.6   ASFAL (اسفل ): ASAFLA (اسفلتہ ) means mean people such as DOON (دون ) and ASFAL (اسفل ) means low and worthless .  It is in Qur’an

 

(Surat-Ham-m-Sajda) (H-m Sajda) سورة حم السجدہ 41:29

  نَجْعَلْهُمَا تَحْتَ اَقْدَامِنَا لِيَكُوْنَا مِنَ الْاَسْفَلِيْنَ                   

  We may put them under our feet that they will be among the lowest.

4.7   KHASI  (خاسی ): KHASAA (خسا ) means tiredness and weakness of eye and KHASI MAN-AL KHANAZEER AND ALKALAAB (خاسی من الخنازیروالکلاب )means scolded dogs and pigs who are not allowed to come close to people .  Allah says:

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - سورة البقرة 2:65

  وَلَقَدْ عَلِمْتُمُ الَّذِيْنَ اعْتَدَوْا مِنْكُمْ فِى السَّبْتِ فَقُلْنَا لَھُمْ كُوْنُوْا قِرَدَةً خٰسِـــِٕيْنَ                    

 And you had already known about those who transgressed among you concerning the sabbath, and We said to them, "Be apes, despised."

 

 

Summary:

4.1          ZALEEL: antonym of AZEEZ (عزيز ) means Great.

4.2          MAHEEN: ZALEEL:  who has fallen from people’s eyes because of his evil deeds .

4.3          SAGHIR: who has fallen from superiority and become subordinate and who accepts it.

4.4          DAKHIR: a subordinate due to the lack of wisdom. Disgraced and incapable.

4.5          ARZAL: disgraced, mean, of low cast, not to be loved, who is low in all aspects.

4.6          ASFAL: low and worthless as compared to superior.

4.7          KHASI: Scolded or most disgraced.
