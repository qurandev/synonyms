4- DARAANA [To Frighten]

Qur’an has referred to it with words like KHAWF  (خوف ) , HADHARA (حذرا ), ARHABA (ارھبا ),  ANDHARA ( أنذر ) , AWA’ADA (ا وعدا ). KHAWFA  (خوف ) , HADHARA (حذرا ), ARHABA (ارھبا ) are already discussed above. And it becomes more mutaadi from necessary. Take a look at their examples:

 

 

Page-521

4.1 Surat Az-Zumar (The Troops) - سورة الزمر 39:36

اَلَيْسَ اللّٰهُ بِكَافٍ عَبْدَهٗ ۭ وَيُخَوِّفُوْنَكَ بِالَّذِيْنَ مِنْ دُوْنِهٖ ۭ         

Is not Allah sufficient for His Servant [Prophet Muhammad]? And [yet], they threaten you with those [they worship] other than Him.

 

4.2 Surat 'Āli `Imrān (Family of Imran) - سورة آل عمران 3:28

 وَيُحَذِّرُكُمُ اللّٰهُ نَفْسَهٗ  ۭ

And Allah warns you of Himself.

 

 

4.3 Surat Al-'Anfāl (The Spoils of War) - سورة الأنفال 8:60

 وَاَعِدُّوْا لَهُمْ مَّا اسْـتَـطَعْتُمْ مِّنْ قُوَّةٍ وَّمِنْ رِّبَاطِ الْخَيْلِ تُرْهِبُوْنَ بِهٖ عَدُوَّ اللّٰهِ وَعَدُوَّكُمْ

And prepare against them whatever you are able of power and of steeds of war by which you may terrify the enemy of Allah and your enemy.

 

And ISTARHAB (إستر هب):  meaning to try scaring somebody or creating a situation to make him afraid. It is mentioned in Qur’an

 

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - سورة الأعراف 7:116

فَلَمَّآ اَلْقَوْا سَحَرُوْٓا اَعْيُنَ النَّاسِ وَاسْتَرْهَبُوْهُمْ وَجَاۗءُوْ بِسِحْرٍ عَظِيْمٍ        

"Throw," and when they threw, they bewitched the eyes of the people and struck terror into them, and they presented a great [feat of] magic.

 

4.4 ANDHARA (أنذر):  means to make aware, to inform and to warn of consequences. To be afraid of the resulting judgement of deeds and accountability on the Day of Judgement. And this is only for Allah and his messengers.  NADHEER (نذير ) means one who warns (antonym is one who give good news, to make afraid by specifying information of fear. And INDHAAR (انذار ) actually is a great favour from MUNDHAR (منذر ).

 

Surat Al-'An`ām (The Cattle) - سورة الأنعام 6:131

اَلَمْ يَاْتِكُمْ رُسُلٌ مِّنْكُمْ يَقُصُّوْنَ عَلَيْكُمْ اٰيٰتِيْ وَيُنْذِرُوْنَكُمْ  لِقَاۗءَ يَوْمِكُمْ ھٰذَا

Did there not come to you messengers from among you, relating to you My verses and warning you of the meeting of this Day of yours?

 

4.5 A’AWADAH (ا عودة): is a common word for promise means to give hope to somebody. But WAEED (وعيد ) denotes the bad result because of a wrong doing, or its punishment, threat. And AOADA (ا وعدا )means to give threat, to intimidate, whether it is verbal or practical, to provide such material. Allah says

 

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - سورة الأعراف 7:86

 وَلَا تَقْعُدُوْا بِكُلِّ صِرَاطٍ تُوْعِدُوْنَ وَتَصُدُّوْنَ عَنْ سَبِيْلِ اللّٰهِ

And do not sit on every path, threatening and averting from the way of Allah those who believe in Him.

 

Summary:

4.1  KHAWAFA: To warn somebody of an upcoming danger.

4.2  HAZARA: To warn somebody of a dangerous thing, to save or to inform.

4.3  ARHABA: To create fear and anxiety.

4.4  INZAAR: Warning from prophets and messengers regarding deeds result and accountability.

4.5  To make afraid, threaten or to give threat.
