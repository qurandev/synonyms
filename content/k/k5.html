5- KAAM ANA [to be useful, to be helpful]

Synonyms: JAZA, AGHNAA

5.1 JAZA (جَزَا) YAJZEE (یَجْزِیْ) JAZA’AN (جَزَاءً): 1. To repay back equally and then set free or save it. 2. To be a substitute, to be helpful. For instance

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:48

 

 يَوۡمً۬ا لَّا تَجۡزِى نَفۡسٌ عَن نَّفۡسٍ۬ شَيۡـًٔ۬ا

a Day when no soul will avail another in the least

And for requiting and saving:

Surah Al-Mumenoon (The believers): سُوۡرَةُ المؤمنون 23:111

 إِنِّى جَزَيۡتُهُمُ ٱلۡيَوۡمَ بِمَا صَبَرُوٓاْ أَنَّهُمۡ هُمُ ٱلۡفَآٮِٕزُونَ

Today, I have rewarded them for their endurance. They are the ones who are the triumphant.

5.2 AGHNAA (اغنٰی): Something being ample and helpful, and removes the need for anything else

Surah Al-Haqqah (The reality): سورۃ الحَاقَّۃ 69:28

مَآ أَغۡنَىٰ عَنِّى مَالِيَهۡۜ  هَلَكَ عَنِّى سُلۡطَـٰنِيَهۡ

My money cannot avail me. My power has vanished from me.”

 

Summary:

5.1 JAZA: To be helpful and save from difficulty

5.2 AGHNAA: Helpful and beneficial.
