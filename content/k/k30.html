30 KAHAN [where]

Synonyms: AYNA, AYNAMA, ANNA

30.1 AYNA (اَیْنَ), AYNAMA (اَیْنَمَا): AYNA (اَیْنَ) is an adverb of place. Used to inquire about a place. For instance

Surat An-Nahl (The Bee): سورۃ النّحل16:27

 أَيۡنَ شُرَڪَآءِىَ ٱلَّذِينَ

 “Where are My associates

MA (مَا) after AYNA (اَیْنَ) gives the meaning of ‘where ever’. Thus the sentence becomes a challenge and requires recompense. For instance

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:78

 أَيۡنَمَا تَكُونُواْ يُدۡرِككُّمُ ٱلۡمَوۡتُ

Wherever you may be, death will catch up with you

 And

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:115

 فَأَيۡنَمَا تُوَلُّواْ فَثَمَّ وَجۡهُ ٱللَّهِ‌ۚ

So wherever you [might] turn, there is the Face of Allah

 THUMMA (ثُمَّ) is actually reply to AYNA (اَیْنَ) - AYNA (اَیْنَ) means ‘where’ and THUMMA (ثُمَّ) mean ‘there’ or ‘that place’.

 

30.2 ANNA (اَنّٰی): Used to inquire about place and circumstances or conditions. In the Quran

Surah Ale- Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:37

 قَالَ يَـٰمَرۡيَمُ أَنَّىٰ لَكِ هَـٰذَا‌ۖ

He said, “O Mary, where did you get this from?”

And when representing an adverb then it gives the meaning of ‘where’. For instance in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:30

قَـٰتَلَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ‌ۚ أَنَّىٰ يُؤۡفَڪُونَ

May God assail them! How (Where) deceived they are!

Summary:

30.1 AYNA: To inquire about a place

30.1 ANNA: To inquire about place as well as circumstances and condition
