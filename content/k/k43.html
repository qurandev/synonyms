43. KYA (What)

Synonyms: A’, HAL(letter),  MA( As a Noun), MAZA.

 

43.1 A’(ءَ): In the meaning of “What”, the use of this letter is common. It is used in all; noun, verb and alphabet. In the Quran:

1. Surat 'Āli `Imrān (Family of Imran) - سورة آل عمران 3:144

 أَفإِنْ مّاتَ أَوْ قُتِلَ

So if he was to die or be killed.

2. Surat Al-'Anbyā' (The Prophets) - سورة الأنبياء 21:62

أَنتَ فَعَلْتَ هَٰذَا

(O Ibrahim A.S) Have you done this?

 

43.2 HAL(هَل): Interrogative pronoun. Is inserted in noun and verb. Does not apply on alphabets. In the Quran:

“Surat Ar-Ra`d (The Thunder) - سورة الرعد 13:16

هَلْ يَسْتَوِي الْأَعْمَىٰ وَالْبَصِيرُ

Is the blind equivalent to the seeing?

 

 

43.3 MA(مَا): (As a noun, an Interrogative). Only comes for irrational creatures. In the Quran:

Surat Al-'Anbyā' (The Prophets) - سورة الأنبياء 21:52

مَا هَٰذِهِ التَّمَاثِيلُ الَّتِي أَنتُمْ لَهَا عَاكِفُونَ

What are these statues to which you are devoted?

 

43.4 MAZA(مَاذَا): ZA(ذَا) when added with MA(مَا )(interrogative pronoun), creates expansion in it’s meaning. It gives meaning of both “what and which”. It can be used for rational creatures as well. In the Quran:

1. Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - سورة البقرة 2:219

يَسْأَلُونَكَ مَاذَا يُنفِقُونَ

They ask you what they should spend.

2. Surat Al-Qaşaş (The Stories) - سورة القصص 28:65

 مَاذَا أَجَبْتُمُ الْمُرْسَلِينَ

What did you answer the messengers?
