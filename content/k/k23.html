23 KINARA [brim, edge, coast]

Synonyms: TARF, HADD, HARF, UFQ, AQTAAR, ARJAA’, SHAFA, MA’ZAL, SHATEE’, SAHIL, AQSA, UDWAH, SADAF

23.1 TARF (طَرْف):  Means end limit, brim, corners of a cubical object, sharp edges, sight or direction (dictionary al Manjad). (Plural ATRAAF (اَطْرَاف)) Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:114

 وَأَقِمِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ طَرَفَىِ ٱلنَّہَارِ

Perform the prayer at the borders of the day,

23.2 HADD (حَدّ): HADDA-ASAKEEN (حَدَّٓالسکین) means to sharpen a knife. HADD-US-SAIF (حَدُّالسَّیْف) means edge of a sword. HADD (حَدّ) means the limit between two things. HADD-UL-MAKAN (حَدُّالمَکَان) means the marking the boundaries of a house. HADDUD ARBA’ (حدود اربعہ) is also from this word which means the four boundaries. Therefore HADD (حَدّ) are the boundaries and limits of something that separates it from other. And HUDDUD ALLAH (حُدُوْدُ الله) means restrictive ordinance of Allah which is a sin to be transgressed. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:229

 وَمَن يَتَعَدَّ حُدُودَ ٱللَّهِ فَأُوْلَـٰٓٮِٕكَ هُمُ ٱلظَّـٰلِمُونَ

Those who transgress God’s limits are the unjust.

 

 23.3 HARF (حَرْف): HARF-UL-JABAL (حَرْفُ الجَبَل) means a sharp end of a mountain. A loftiest mountain. It is said FULANUN-ALA’-HARFIM-MIN-AMRIHI “فُلَانٌ عَلٰی حَرْفٍ مِّنْ امرہ” means the person is as the brink of the matter. Meaning if anything is done against his will he will break away (dictionary al Manjad). Limit of agitation. Allah says in the Quran.

Surah Al-Hajj  (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:11

  وَمِنَ ٱلنَّاسِ مَن يَعۡبُدُ ٱللَّهَ عَلَىٰ حَرۡفٍ۬‌ۖ فَإِنۡ أَصَابَهُ ۥ خَيۡرٌ ٱطۡمَأَنَّ بِهِۦ‌ۖ وَإِنۡ أَصَابَتۡهُ فِتۡنَةٌ ٱنقَلَبَ عَلَىٰ وَجۡهِهِۦ

And among the people is he who worships God on edge. When something good comes his way, he is content with it. But when an ordeal strikes him, he makes a turnaround.

23.4 UFQ (اُفْق): AFAQ (اَفَقَ) Two things that are wide apart and meet together at the edge or seem like they meet, horizon. The place where they meet is called UFAQ (اُفْق) for instance the sky and earth are far apart. At the far end of sight where they seem like meeting each other is called UFAQ (اُفْق). (Plural AFAAQ (اٰفاق)). AFAAQ (اٰفاق) means the expanse of universe which covers a huge area. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah An-Najm (the star): سورۃ النَّجم 53:7

وَهُوَ بِٱلۡأُفُقِ ٱلۡأَعۡلَىٰ

 While he was at the highest horizon

In another place in the Holy Quran

Surah Fussilat (Explained in Detail): سورۃ فُصّلَت 41:53

سَنُرِيهِمۡ ءَايَـٰتِنَا فِى ٱلۡأَفَاقِ وَفِىٓ أَنفُسِہِمۡ

We will show them Our proofs on the horizons, and in their very souls

23.5 AQTAAR (اَقْطَار): QATARA (قَطَرَ) means to stand in a line or row. QATAR-AL-BAEERU-ILAL-BAEER (قَطَرَ ٓالْبَعِیْرُ اِلٰی الْبَعِیْر) means to line up all the camels in a row. AQTAR-UD-DUNIYA (اقطار الدنیا) means the 4 corners of earth. In Geometry it is the line passing through the middle that divides the earth into two equal parts i.e. the equator (dictionary al Manjad). Thus QATAR (قطر) refers to a straight line and also to a circular object. And there can be multiple such lines in every direction. Allah says in the Quran

Suran Al-Ahzaab (The clans): سورۃ الأحزَاب 33:14

وَلَوۡ دُخِلَتۡ عَلَيۡہِم مِّنۡ أَقۡطَارِهَا ثُمَّ سُٮِٕلُواْ ٱلۡفِتۡنَةَ لَأَتَوۡهَا وَمَا تَلَبَّثُواْ بِہَآ إِلَّا يَسِيرً۬ا

Had it been invaded from its sides, and they were asked to dissent, they would have done so with little hesitation.

In another place in the Holy Quran

Surah Ar-Rahman (The Beneficent): سُوۡرَةُ الرَّحمٰن 55:33

يَـٰمَعۡشَرَ ٱلۡجِنِّ وَٱلۡإِنسِ إِنِ ٱسۡتَطَعۡتُمۡ أَن تَنفُذُواْ مِنۡ أَقۡطَارِ ٱلسَّمَـٰوَٲتِ وَٱلۡأَرۡضِ فَٱنفُذُواْ‌ۚ

O society of jinn and humans! If you can pass through the bounds of the heavens and the earth, go ahead and pass

 

23.6 ARJAA’ (اَرْجَاء): (singular RAJA (رَجَا)) Means an end of a circular object. RAJA-AL-BIR’ (رَجَا الْبِئْر) means edge of a well. RAJA-AL-SAMAA’ (رَجَاالسَّمَاء) means edge of the sky. In the Quran

Surah Al-Haqqah (The reality): سورۃ الحَاقَّۃ 69:17

 وَٱلۡمَلَكُ عَلَىٰٓ أَرۡجَآٮِٕهَا‌ۚ وَيَحۡمِلُ عَرۡشَ رَبِّكَ فَوۡقَهُمۡ يَوۡمَٮِٕذٍ۬ ثَمَـٰنِيَةٌ۬

 And the angels will be ranged around its borders, while eight will be carrying the Throne of your Lord above them that Day.

23.7 SHAFA (شَفَا): This word is commonly used for near death state. SHAFA (شَفَا) refers to edge or brink of something that is hollow or empty and standing on which causes a fear of falling in and death. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ale- Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:103

 وَكُنتُمۡ عَلَىٰ شَفَا حُفۡرَةٍ۬ مِّنَ ٱلنَّارِ فَأَنقَذَكُم مِّنۡہَا‌ۗ

And you were on the brink of a pit of fire, and He saved you from it.

In another place in Holy Quran Allah says

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:109

 أَم مَّنۡ أَسَّسَ بُنۡيَـٰنَهُ ۥ عَلَىٰ شَفَا جُرُفٍ هَارٍ۬

or he who founds his structure on the brink of a cliff that is about to tumble

23.8 MA’ZAL(مَعْزل): AZAL (مَعْزل) means avoidance or withdrawal, to separate. To turn aside and get saved.  According to Ibn Al Faras “تدُلُّ علی تنخیة وامالة وابعاد” means MA’ZAL is that edge or side turning to which a man is saved from a peril. Allah says in the Holy Quran    

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:42

 وَنَادَىٰ نُوحٌ ٱبۡنَهُ ۥ وَڪَانَ فِى مَعۡزِلٍ۬ يَـٰبُنَىَّ ٱرۡڪَب مَّعَنَا

and Noah called out to his son, who had separated himself (from the rest): "O my son! embark with us,

23.9 SHATEE’ (شاطئ): Means one of the two edges of something. One of the side or coast of a river or a valley. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Qasas (The stories): سورۃ القَصَص 28:30

 فَلَمَّآ أَتَٮٰهَا نُودِىَ مِن شَـٰطِىِٕ ٱلۡوَادِ ٱلۡأَيۡمَنِ

When he reached it, he was called from the right side of the valley,

23.10 SAHIL (سَاحِل): This word is used particularly for a coast or shore of a river or sea (dictionary al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Ta-Ha (Ta-ha): سورۃ طٰھٰ  20:39

 فَلۡيُلۡقِهِ ٱلۡيَمُّ بِٱلسَّاحِلِ

The river will wash him to shore,

 

23.11 AQSA (اَقْصَا): QSW (قصو) has the perception of being far or living far. And AQSA (اَقْصٰی) means a far end or the other side. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Yaseen (Yaseen): سورۃ ٰیٰس 36:20

وَجَآءَ مِنۡ أَقۡصَا ٱلۡمَدِينَةِ رَجُلٌ۬ يَسۡعَىٰ

 Then there came running, from the farthest part of the City

23.12 UDWAH (عُدْوَۃ): A’DA (عَدَا) means a traveler going far away and also edge of a valley. UDWAH (عُدْوَۃ) means a high place on the edge of a valley (dictionary al Manjad). Thus UDWAH (عُدْوَۃ) refers to that edge of a valley that is not just far but higher in altitude. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Anfal (The Spoils of War): سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنفَال 8:42

 إِذۡ أَنتُم بِٱلۡعُدۡوَةِ ٱلدُّنۡيَا وَهُم بِٱلۡعُدۡوَةِ ٱلۡقُصۡوَىٰ

Remember you were on the hither side of the valley, and they on the farther side

 

23.13 SADAF (صَدَف): An open field in the middle of mountains is called a valley. And the edge or boundary of that valley is known as SADAF (صَدَف). So SADAF (صَدَف) can be translated as an “edge of a valley” but “edge of mountain” is a better translation. Thus Imam Raghib also refers to it as “edge of mountain”. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:96

 حَتَّىٰٓ إِذَا سَاوَىٰ بَيۡنَ ٱلصَّدَفَيۡنِ

At length, when he had filled up the space between the two steep mountain-sides

Summary:

23.1 TARF: Extreme edge or limit of anything

23.2 HADD:  Boundaries and limits of something that separates it from other

23.3 HARF: Sharp edge, Limit of agitation

23.4 UFQ: The edge where two separate things meet.

23.5 AQTAAR: Side or edge of a circular thing.

23.6 ARJAA’: Edge of a circular object i.e. a well or sky

23.7 SHAFA: Edge of something a that hollow, and falling into it can cause death.

23.8 MA’ZAL: A edge of side which serves as a shelter or protection

23.9 SHATEE’: One of the edge of a river or field

23.10 SAHIL: River or sea coast

23.11 AQSA: The other edge or side

12.12 UDWAH: A far away lofty edge or side.  

12.13 SADAF: Edge of a mountain.
