3 KAGHAZ [paper]

Synonyms: RAQQ, QIRTAAS

3.1 RAQQ (رَقّ): RAQQA (رَقَّ) means to be thin and soft. RIQQAT (رَقَّت) means to be soft hearted/ kind hearted (opposite QASAAWAT (قساوت)). RAQQ (رَقّ) is anything that is thin and soft, for instance a leaf, a membrane, or a thin piece of leather or paper scroll. Thus this word has a very ordinary use. Allah says in the Quran

Surah At-Tur (The Mount): سورۃ الطُّور 52:2

 وَكِتَـٰبٍ۬ مَّسۡطُورٍ۬ فِى رَقٍّ۬ مَّنشُورٍ۬

 And a Book inscribed. In a published scroll.

 

3.2 QIRTAAS (قِرْطَاس): Means a handwritten letter (dictionary Al Manjad), regardless of how many pages. QIRTAAS ABYAD (قرطاس ابیض) is a famous word which means a complete and published report on some important matter. QIRTAAS (قِرْطَاس) actually refers to that initial form of paper invented by Egyptians, during the time of revelation of Quran this paper was available but was very scarce. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The Cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:7

 وَلَوۡ نَزَّلۡنَا عَلَيۡكَ كِتَـٰبً۬ا فِى قِرۡطَاسٍ۬ فَلَمَسُوهُ بِأَيۡدِيہِمۡ

Had We sent down upon you a book on paper, and they had touched it with their hands,
