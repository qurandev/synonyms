17- Few

Words used are ‘ma’adudah’(معدودہ), ‘biza’a’( بضع), ‘nafar’(نفر).

1)             ‘ma’adudah’(معدودہ): is meant for counting. Single or few, many in comparison of less. For example hundred is less than thousand. For example: And they sold him for a reduced price - a few dirhams. مَعْدُودَةٍ دَرَاهِمَ بَخْسٍ بِثَمَنٍ وَشَرَوْهُ[12:20]

2)             ‘biza’a’( بضع): include countings from three to ten. It includes only odd numbers from 3 to 9. For example:

  But they, after their defeat, will overcome. Within three to nine years. سِنِينَ بِضْعِ فِي سَيَغْلِبُونَ غَلَبِهِمْ بَعْدِ مِّن وَهُم[30:4]

3) ‘nafar’(نفر): ‘An-nafaru’ means all people as well as a group of from 3 to 10 peaple. ‘salaasatu nafarun aw anfaazin’ means 3 men. For example:        Say, [O Muhammad], "It has been revealed to me that a group of the jinn listened and said, 'Indeed, we have heard an amazing Qur'an.  عَجَبًقُرْآنًا سَمِعْنَاإِنَّا فَقَالُوا الْجِنِّ مِّنَ نَفَرٌ اسْتَمَعَ أَنَّهُ إِلَيَّ أُوحِيَ قُلْ [72:1]

Conclusion:

1)            ‘ma’adudah’(معدودہ): few in numbers. Less as compared to many.

2)            ‘biza’a’( بضع): 3 to 10, for general things.

3)            ‘nafar’(نفر):group of 3 to 10.

18- To Select

Words used are ‘ikhtaar’(اختار (خیر)), ‘istafaa’( اصطفی(صفو), ‘ijtabaa’(اجتبی(جبو)), ‘istakhlasa’( استخلص (خلص)), ‘istana’a’( اصطنع(صنع).

1)        ‘ikhtaar’(اختار (خیر)): ‘khair’ means something desired by everyone and ‘ikhtaar’ means to select the best one among the items of same type.

        For example: And Moses chose from his people seventy men for Our appointment. لِّمِيقَاتِنَا رَجُلًا سَبْعِينَ قَوْمَه مُوسَىٰ وَاخْتَارَ[7:155]

2)        ‘istafaa’( اصطفی(صفو): ‘as-safa’ and ‘safaw’ means to be neat and pure. ‘as-safiyyu’ and ‘as-safwah’ means a loyal friend. ‘istafaa’ to select something on the basis of its purification.

              Quran says: Indeed, Allah chose Adam and Noah and the family of Abraham and the family of 'Imran over the worlds

               إِنَّ اللَّهَ اصْطَفَىٰ آدَمَ وَنُوحًا وَآلَ إِبْرَاهِيمَ وَآلَ عِمْرَانَ عَلَى الْعَالَمِينَ

3)         ‘ijtabaa’(اجتبی(جبو)): ‘jabaw’ means 1)to collect something and then 2)to take it somewhere. According to Ibn faris it means the collection of taxex and revenues including to carry it to treasure. ‘mujbaa’ means tax. Quran says:

        Have we not established for them a safe sanctuary to which are brought the fruits of all things as provision from Us? شَيْءٍ كُلِّ ثَمَرَاتُ إِلَيْهِ يُجْبَىٰ [28:57]

   And ‘ijtabaa’ means to select a good thing and make it closer, to choose it. Quran says: And thus will your      Lord  choose you and teach you the interpretation of narratives. الْأَحَادِيثِ  تَأْوِيلِ مِن وَيُعَلِّمُكَ رَبُّكَ يَجْتَبِيكَ لِكَ كَذَ‌ٰوَ [12:6]

4)        ‘istakhlasa’( استخلص (خلص)): ‘khalasa’(derive from ‘khalata’) means to be clear and exclusive from the cast of other things. And ‘khallasa’ means to make exclusive, safi is different from it as it may be already the exclusive one. Hence ‘istakhlasa’ means to select someone and make it reserve for own.

Quran says: And the king said, "Bring him to me; I will appoint him exclusively for myself.".  ۖيلِنَفْسِ أَسْتَخْلِصْهُ بِهِ ائْتُونِي الْمَلِكُ وَقَالَ [12:54]

5)        ‘istana’a’( اصطنع(صنع): ‘sana’a’ means to create something with artistic skills. ‘istana’a’ means selecting someone special and appoint him for an important purpose.

Allah says for Moses: And I produced you for Myself. يلِنَفْسِ وَاصْطَنَعْتُكَ[20:41]

Conclusion:

1)            ‘ikhtaar’(اختار (خیر)): to select the best one from different things.

2)            ‘istafaa’( اصطفی(صفو): to select someone on the basis of its purification.

3)            ‘ijtabaa’(اجتبی(جبو)):selecting someone and make it own.

4)            ‘istakhlasa’( استخلص (خلص)):selecting someone special for a special purpose.

5)             ‘istana’a’( اصطنع(صنع): selecting and appointing someone special for a special purpose.
