26  CHORNA      [ To leave ]

SYNONYMS: TARAKA, HAJARA, 3ATTALA, KHALLAA, YAZAR, DA3U, and GHAADAR

 

 

26.1  TARAKA (تَرَكَ) : this is a common word. It means to leave something, whether it is by purpose or unintentionally, thoughtfully or impulsively. It can be used in any of these cases.

Refer to 2:180 - this case is unintentional.

Refer to 12:17 - this case is thoughtful.

____________________________________

26.2  HAJARA (هَجَرَ) : used when one person cuts off his association with another person or people.

Refer to 73:10

Also, another instance of cutting off relations with people is to separate from the place itself. In this context, the meaning of هجرت is to leave the country.

Refer to 2:218

____________________________________

26.3 3ATTALA (عَطَّلَ) : to leave something as useless or to make a worker useless. تعطيل means the time when one gets free from work. And مُعَطّل is that worker who is working now but he is being stopped from working.

Refer to 81:4

And مُعَطَّلَةٌ بِئْرٌ refers to well which has been destroyed, is empty or unpopulated.

Refer to 22:45

_____________________________________

26.4  KHALLAA (خَلّٰى) : خَلَا means to be empty. It can be used in both, (place or tongue**********). خلوت means alone. بعض الٰى خلا means to meet someone alone. خَلّٰى means to separate yourself from someone and leave them alone, ie leaving someone in an empty place. It means to give someone freedom, and not to hold on to them. سَبِيْلاً خَلّٰى is a saying which refers not to hold on to someone.

Refer to 9:5

_____________________________________

26.5  YAZAR (يذر) : it is from (Afaal e Naaqsa. It is only used in MUDAARE and AMAR**************). It is used in two ways:

1) to leave something because it is insignificant.

Refer to 6:70

2) doing something and then not allowing anyone to address any problems or interferences in it. In English it is used as the word “let”. For example, “let me do this”.

Refer to 74:11

Here it means that it is between Me and him. You don’t interfere in this matter.

_____________________________________

26.6  DA3U (دَعُ) : this also (Faal Naaqas*********************). It means to leave something peacefully.

Refer to 33:48

And وَدَّعَ means to say goodbye to a traveler, or to see someone off happily and with respect. It also includes getting separated yourself and leaving that person.

Refer to 93:31

_____________________________________

26.7 GHAADARA (غَادَرَ) : the word غَدَر is used for breaking a promise or unfaithfulness. And غدار means an unfaithful person, or a promise breaker. And غَادَرَ means to leave something because of some fault in it, or to leave something out.

Refer to 18:47

This means any person, no matter how his deeds are, will not be left out.

Refer to 18:50

_____________________________________

 

SUMMARY

 

TARAKA: is a common word

HAJARA: to leave your relations or country

3ATTALA: to leave something useless

KHALLAA: to leave someone’s way, not hold on to them.

YAZAR: to leave something because it is insignificant.

DA3U: means to leave someone peacefully

GHAADAR: to leave someone because of some fault.
