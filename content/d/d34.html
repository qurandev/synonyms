34. Giving - Dena - (دينا)  *

Synonyms:-

Ata (*اٰتٰی*), Aata (*اَعْطَى*), Ada (*اَدَاء*), Deyatun (*دِيَة*), Asaaba (

*اَثَاب*), Wahaba (*وَهَبَ*), Rafada (*رَفَدَ*), DafaAa (Ila) (*(**اِلٰی**)

**دَفَعَ*), Hadeyyat (*هَدِيِّة*), Nahala (*نَحَلَ*)

*34.1. Ata (اٰتٰی): *To give someone something.

*Quranic Example:-*

“…and *gives* wealth, in spite of love for it…” (2:177)

*34.2. Aata* (*اَعْطَى*)*: *Two meanings.**

1). To give someone something as a favor to them. To something bestow upon

someone *(Mufraddat)*.

*Quranic Example:-*

“He said, “Our Lord is He who *gave* each thing its form and then guided

[it].”” (20:50)

2). To be loyal to someone and to serve them *(Munjid)*.* *And therefore it

also means to give someone a reward greater than their service or hard work.

*Quranic Example:-*

“And as for those who were [destined to be] prosperous, they will be in

Paradise, abiding therein as long as the heavens and the earth endure,

except what your Lord should will – a *bestowal* uninterrupted.” (11:108)

*34.3. Ada (**اَدَاء**): *To give someone their due right in full. This

word is usually used in “give & take” situations such as with a deposit,

loan or a tribute.

*Quranic Example:-***

“And if one of you *entrusts* another, then let him who is entrusted

discharge his trust [faithfully] and let him fear Allah, his Lord.” (2:283)

*34.4. Deyatun (**دِيَة**):* Used to mean blood money but specifically

payment/ransom for someone who was unintentionally killed.

*Quranic Example:-*

“And whoever kills a believer by mistake – then the freeing of a believing

slave and a *compensation payment* presented to the deceased’s family [is

required] unless they give [up their right as] charity.” (4:92)

*34.5. Asaaba (**اَثَابَ**): *A reward or return for any sort of act good

or bad, although usually it is used for a return for good acts. Also this

word contains some characteristics of *34.1.* *Aata **(*اَعْطَى*)* i.e. to

give the return equal to or more than what the act deserves.

*Quranic Example:-*

“So Allah *rewarded* them for what they said with gardens [in Paradise]

beneath which rivers flow, wherein they abide eternally.” (5:85)

*34.6. Wahaba (**وَهَبَ**): *To give someone something not as a return and

without anything done by them to deserve it, and without any expectation of

receiving anything in return for it.

*Quranic Example:-*

“He gives to whom He wills female [children], and He gives to whom He wills

males.” (42:49)

*34.7. Rafada (**رَفَدَ**):* Its meaning is based on two things; ‘to give’

and ‘to help’. It could mean like to give the poor something as a donation,

or, the giving of or appointing of allowance to someone. *Rafadah *(*رفادہ*)

is the name of that fund that the Quraish used to keep for those hopeful

for performing Pilgrimage (*hajj*) but were too impoverished to do so. And *

Arfada* (*اَرْفَدَ*) means to establish an amount of donation for someone

such that they keep taking their desired amount from that appointed amount *

(Mufraddat)*. And *Rafd* (*رفد*) means donation.

*Quranic Example:-*

“And they were followed in this [world] with a curse and on the Day of

Resurrection. And wretched is the *gift* which is given.” (11:99)

*34.8. DafaAa (Ila) ((**اِلٰی**) **دَفَعَ**): * *DafaAa* (*دَفَعَ* ) means

that for the protection of something keeping any external dangers and

attacks away *(Mufraddat)*.  And when it is used with *Ila (**اِلٰی**) *then

that means to hand over someone their possession.

*Quranic Example:-*

“And test the orphans [in their abilities] until they reach marriageable

age. Then if you perceive in them sound judgement, release their property

to them.” (4:6)

*34.9. Hadeyyat (**هَدِيِّة**): *These are those gifts we give each other

as a welcome or upon the pleasure of meeting them. And *Mehda* (*مِهْدٰى*)

is the thing in which this gift is put in to give.

*Quranic Example:-*

“But what Allah has given me is better than what He has given you. Rather,

it is you who rejoice in your gift.” (27:36)

*34.10. Nahala* *(**نَحَلَ**): *To give someone something. * **Nehal Almira*

*نَحَلَ الْمِرْاة *is giving a bride her promissory gift from her in-laws

at the time of marriage. And *Nahal* ((*نَحل* is compensation* (Munjid)*.

Therefore, *Al'nahlat* (*الْنَّحْلَة*) and *Al'nihlat* (*الْنِّحْلَة*) are

both used for a compensation that is given without the hopes of getting

something in return – that is the meaning of *Tabran

*(*تَبرعاً*).Therefore, both the words are associated with

*Hiba* (*ہبہ*) which is a gift. And any kind of *Hiba* or gift can be

called *Nihlat* (*نِحْلَة*), but any kind of *Nihlat* cannot be called *Hiba

*, because all compensations are not gifts but, all gifts are

compensations, without demanding something in return.

Summary:-

Ata (*اٰتٰی*):  To give. Used commonly.

Aata (*اَعْطَى*): A reward for a service or hard work given greater that

what it deserves.

Ada (*اَدَاءٌ*): To give someone their due right in full.

Deyat (*دِيَة*): Payment of blood money.

Asaaba (*اَثَابَ*): Similar to* Aata* (*اَعْطَى*). To give a return for a

work more than what it deserves.

Wahab (*وَهب*): To give something not as a return or reward and without

expectation of receiving a return for it.

Rafada (*رَفَدَ*): To help the poor or destitute by giving them a donation.

DafaAa (Ila) (*(**اِلٰی**) **دَفَعَ*): To hand over someone their

possession.

Hadeyyat (*هَدِيَّة*): Those gifts we give each other as a welcome or upon

the pleasure of meeting them.

Nehlatan (*نِحْلَةَ*): A compensation given without hopes of getting

something in return.
