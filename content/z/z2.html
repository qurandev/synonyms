2. ZABARDASTI KERNA [to force, to coerce]

Synonyms: AKRAHA , JABARA, QAHARA, SAKH-KHARA, RAHIQA

2.1 AKRAHA (اَکْرَہَ): KARIHA (کَرِہَ) means to abhor, detest or loathe something. AKRAHA (اَکْرَہَ) means to coerce or compel someone. It basically refers to two things: loathing and coercing. In the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ 2:256

لَآ إِكۡرَاهَ فِى ٱلدِّينِ*ۖ

Let there be no compulsion in religion

Another reference in Quran:

Surah Al-Noor (The light): سورۃ النور 24:33

وَلَا تُكۡرِهُواْ فَتَيَـٰتِكُمۡ عَلَى ٱلۡبِغَآءِ إِنۡ أَرَدۡنَ تَحَصُّنً۬ا لِّتَبۡتَغُواْ عَرَضَ ٱلۡحَيَوٰةِ ٱلدُّنۡيَا*ۚ

And do not compel your girls to prostitution, seeking the materials of this life, if they desire to remain chaste

2.2 JABARA (جَبَرَ): the meaning also consists of two parts: 1. Coercion, 2. Rectification. Essentially to rectify by force. JABAR-AL-ADHM (جَبَرَالْعَظْم) means to cure (rectify) a broken bone. JABAR-AL- ‘AMR (جَبَرَالاَمْر) means to compel someone to perform a task. Allah mentions His Attributes in the Quran

Surah Al Hashr (The exile): سورۃ الحَشر 59:23

ٱلۡعَزِيزُ ٱلۡجَبَّارُ ٱلۡمُتَڪَبِّرُ*ۚ

the Almighty, the Omnipotent, the Overwhelming

This word is also used to indicate coercion. For instance in Quran

Surah Ash-Shura’ (The Poets): صورۃ الشُّعْراء 26:130

وَإِذَا بَطَشۡتُم بَطَشۡتُمۡ جَبَّارِينَ

And when you strike, you strike mercilessly?

2.3 QAHARA (قَھَرَ): also consists of two parts: 1. Dominate, 2. Humiliate - to dominate over someone and to humiliate those under influence. To signify strong oppressing a weak. In the Quran

Surah Ad-Duha (The morning hours): سورۃ الضُّحیٰ 93:9

فَأَمَّا ٱلۡيَتِيمَ فَلَا تَقۡهَرۡ وَأَمَّا ٱلسَّآٮِٕلَ فَلَا تَنۡہَرۡ

Therefore, do not mistreat the orphan. Nor rebuff the seeker.

Page 558

2.4 SAKH-KHARA (سَخَّرَ): SAKHARA (سَخَرَ) means to ridicule, and SAKH-KHARA (سَخَّرَ) comprises of two things: 1. Coercion, 2. Self regard. To force someone to your bidding. To coerce someone to work against their will. This word is commonly used when the conquest of universe is deliberated. In the Quran

Surah Ibraheem (Abraham): سورۃ ابراھیم 14:33

وَسَخَّرَ لَكُمُ ٱلشَّمۡسَ وَٱلۡقَمَرَ دَآٮِٕبَيۡنِ*ۖ وَسَخَّرَ لَكُمُ ٱلَّيۡلَ وَٱلنَّہَارَ

And He committed the sun and the moon to your service, both continuously pursuing their courses, and He committed the night and the day to your service

Another reference:

Surah Az-Zukhruf (Ornaments of Gold): سورۃالزُّخرف 43:32

وَرَفَعۡنَا بَعۡضَہُمۡ فَوۡقَ بَعۡضٍ۬ دَرَجَـٰتٍ۬ لِّيَتَّخِذَ بَعۡضُہُم بَعۡضً۬ا سُخۡرِيًّ۬ا*ۗ

We elevated some of them in rank above others, that some of them would take others in service

2.5 RAHIQA (رَھِقَ): to hide, to cover something. RAHIQA-UL-AMR (رَھِقََہُ الْاَمْر) means something forcefully detained him. Thus RAHIQA refers to: 1. Coercion/Force, 2. Hide or cover.

Surah Al-Qalam (The Pen):سورۃ القلم 68:43

خَـٰشِعَةً أَبۡصَـٰرُهُمۡ تَرۡهَقُهُمۡ ذِلَّةٌ۬*ۖ

Their eyes subdued, shame will cover them.

Summary:

2.1 IKRAHA: loathe and coercion.

2.2 JABARA: coerce to rectify.

2.3 QAHARA: coercion and oppression.

2.4 SAKH-KHARA: coercion and self regard

2.5 RAHIQA: coercion and enshroud.
