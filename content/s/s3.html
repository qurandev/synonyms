3 SAAL

[Year]

Synonyms: AAM, SINEEN, HAUL, HIJJAJ

3.1 aam(عَامٌ۬): (pl: awaam (عوام)) the year that has amplitude and abundance meaning the year of peace and prosperity. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Yousuf (Joseph) - سُوۡرَةُ یُوسُف

12:49

ثُمَّ يَأۡتِى مِنۢ بَعۡدِ ذَٲلِكَ عَامٌ۬ فِيهِ يُغَاثُ ٱلنَّاسُ وَفِيهِ يَعۡصِرُونَ

Then, after that, will come a year when the people will have plenteous crops and when they will press (wine and oil)

________________

Similarly, the word aam is also used as a prayer. On occasions of Eid, instead of saying Eid Mubarak, people of Arab greet each other by saying

أنتم و كل عام 

Meaning we wish and pray that your year ahead passes with peace and prosperity.

 

3.2 sanata (سَنَةٍ) (pl. sineen) (سِّنِينَ): the year of hardships. This word has been used for depicting difficulty, drought and starvation. Allah states that:

________________

And we straitened Pharaoh's folk with famine and dearth of fruits, that peradventure they might heed.

________________

And in the following ayah:

فَلَبِثَ فِيهِمۡ أَلۡفَ سَنَةٍ إِلَّا خَمۡسِينَ عَامً۬ا

And verily we sent Noah (as Our messenger) unto his folk, and he continued with them for a thousand years save fifty years; and the flood engulfed them, for they were wrong-doers.

the nine hundred and fifty years that Hazrat Noah passed in hardships against his nation, they have been described by the word sineen. And the fifty years prior to nabuwwat, when there weren’t any hardships or fights, have been described by the word aam. And to keep track of chronology or time and era, the word sanata has been used which also shows that days of prosperity are usually less than days of difficulty and distress. 3.3 haul(haal)(حَوۡلِ): meaning change from one state to another (haal pl halaat) Halaat-u-dahar(حالات الدهر) meaning passing of day and night, revolutions of time and haal-ul-haul(حال الحول) meaning passing of a period of one year and haul meaning the complete cycle of a year i.e. the period of time from a specific date of a year till the same date of next year is called haul. Zakat Is also given according to haul. The matters of breastfeeding and divorce are also in accordance with haul. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - سورة البقرة

2:240

وَصِيَّةً۬ لِّأَزۡوَٲجِهِم مَّتَـٰعًا إِلَى ٱلۡحَوۡلِ غَيۡرَ إِخۡرَاجٍ۬‌ۚ

(In the case of) those of you who are about to die and leave behind them wives, they should bequeath unto their wives a provision for the year without turning them out, but if they go out (of their own accord) there is no sin for you in that which they do of themselves within their rights. Allah is Mighty, Wise.

________________

3.4 hijaj(sg: hajjah)(حِجَج): meaning to come and go repeatedly, to travel frequently. Hajjah(حجة) is derived from the word hajj(حج); and because hajj occurs once each year, a year is sometimes referred to as hajj. In earlier times when there were no calendars and no proper way of keeping track of days, months and years, people usually counted years on the basis of hajj. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Qasas (The Story) - سُوۡرَةُ القَصَص

28:27

إِنِّىٓ أُرِيدُ أَنۡ أُنكِحَكَ إِحۡدَى ٱبۡنَتَىَّ هَـٰتَيۡنِ عَلَىٰٓ أَن تَأۡجُرَنِى ثَمَـٰنِىَ حِجَجٍ۬‌ۖ

He said: Lo! I fain would marry thee to one of these two daughters of mine on condition that thou hirest thyself to me for (the term of) eight pilgrimages. Then if thou completest ten it will be of thine own accord, for I would not make it hard for thee. Allah willing, thou wilt find me of the righteous.

________________

Summary:

1. AAM: the year of peace and prosperity

2. SANAH: the year of drought and starvation

3. HAUL: A complete cycle of year from a specific date, according to lunar calculations.

4. HIJJAJ: The rough way of counting years.

Year

The words عم )عام , ( سنو) سِنِين, حول, حج) have been used.

1- عَام

عَامis considered a year of prosperity and thriving. It says in the Quran:

ثُمَّ يَأۡتِى مِنۢ بَعۡدِ ذَٲلِكَ عَامٌ۬ فِيهِ يُغَاثُ ٱلنَّاسُ وَفِيهِ يَعۡصِرُونَ

“Then, after that, will come a year when the people will have plenteous crops and when they will press (wine and oil)” 12/49

In a similar context, the word عَام has been used as a word of دعَا or prayer. The Arabs used to greet each other on Eid with a prayer saying:

اَنتُم وَّ كُلَّ عََامٍ بِخَير

“May this year bring you peace and tranquillity”

2- سَنَة(Pl: سِنِين)

It means a year of adversity. Also, the same word has been used for drought and famine.

وَلَقَدۡ أَخَذۡنَآ ءَالَ فِرۡعَوۡنَ بِٱلسِّنِينَ

“And we straitened Pharaoh's folk with famine and dearth“ 7/130

فَلَبِثَ فِيهِمۡ أَلۡفَ سَنَةٍ إِلَّا خَمۡسِينَ عَامً۬ا

“He continued with them for a thousand years save fifty years“ 29/14

In this ayah, the 950 years that Nooh (Noah) spent amongst his people in extreme hardship and opposition have been denoted by the word سِنِينand the 50 years before prophet hood, where there was no opposition from his people are denoted by عَام.

The word سنةis normally used to keep and account of time or a period which also points towards the fact that the periods of prosperity are normally a lot less than the periods of adversity.

هُوَ ٱلَّذِى جَعَلَ ٱلشَّمۡسَ ضِيَآءً۬ وَٱلۡقَمَرَ نُورً۬ا وَقَدَّرَهُ ۥ مَنَازِلَ لِتَعۡلَمُواْ عَدَدَ ٱلسِّنِينَ وَٱلۡحِسَابَ*ۚ

“He it is Who appointed the sun a splendour and the moon a light, and measured for her stages, that ye might know the number of the years” 10/5

3- حول

حَالَmeans the transition of times from one state to another. (حَال Pl: حَالاَت).

حََالاَت الدَّهَرmeans the passing of days or transition from day to day. حال الحولmeans the passing of a year and حولmeans one complete rotation of the year from one particular date in a year to the next time or date after one full year. زكوةis calculated and paid by حولand divorce and the period of suckling are also determined by it.

وَصِيَّةً۬ لِّأَزۡوَٲجِهِم مَّتَـٰعًا إِلَى ٱلۡحَوۡلِ غَيۡرَ إِخۡرَاجٍ۬*ۚ

“They should bequeath unto their wives a provision for the year without turning them out” 2/240

4- حِجّج(sing: حَجَّة)

It means something that comes again and again, some even that repeats, that comes a lot and because Hajj comes once a year, every year, hence the year is called Hajj. In old times there were no calendars to account for days, months or even years so people, would commonly count years with reference to the number of Hajjs.

قَالَ إِنِّىٓ أُرِيدُ أَنۡ أُنكِحَكَ إِحۡدَى ٱبۡنَتَىَّ هَـٰتَيۡنِ عَلَىٰٓ أَن تَأۡجُرَنِى ثَمَـٰنِىَ حِجَجٍ۬*ۖ

“He said: Lo! I fain would marry thee to one of these two daughters of mine on condition that thou hirest thyself to me for (the term of) eight pilgrimages” 28/27

Inference

1- عَام: The year of prosperity

2- حول: A whole rotation of the year, starting from a particular date (lunar)

3- سَنَةَ: The year of drought

4- حِجَج: A common way of counting years
