17 SURANG [tunnel]

Synonyms: NAFAQ, SARAB

17.1 NAFAQ (نَفَق): a tunnel that opens at both ends. a subterraneous passage that opens at both ends. Like a tunnel dug through mountain for traffic or trains. NAFEEQ-US-SARAWEEL (نَیْفَقُ السَّرَاوِیْل) means belt of a pajama, NAFIQA’-AL-YARBU’ (نَافِقاء الیَرْبُوْع) means the hole of a wild rat having two openings. NAFAQAL-YARBU’U-WA-NAFAQ (نَافَقَ الیَرْبُوْعُ وَنَفَقَ) means the mouse entered from one end and left from other. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:35

 فَإِنِ ٱسۡتَطَعۡتَ أَن تَبۡتَغِىَ نَفَقً۬ا فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ أَوۡ سُلَّمً۬ا فِى ٱلسَّمَآءِ

If you find their rejection hard to bear, then if you can, seek a tunnel into the earth, or a stairway into the heaven

17.2 SARAB (سَرَب): a tunnel with only one opening. SARABA (سَرَبَ) means to keep on advancing in, and WA-INSARBAT-AL-HAYYAHTU-ILA-HUJARIHA (انسَرَبتِ الْحَیَّۃُ اِلٰی جُحْرِھَا) refers a snake slipping into his hole or burrow. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:61

فَلَمَّا بَلَغَا مَجۡمَعَ بَيۡنِهِمَا نَسِيَا حُوتَهُمَا فَٱتَّخَذَ سَبِيلَهُ ۥ فِى ٱلۡبَحۡرِ سَرَبً۬ا

But when they reached the Junction, they forgot (about) their Fish, which took its course through the sea (straight) as in a tunnel.

Summary:

17.1 NAFAQ: A tunnel with two openings

17.2 SARAB: A tunnel with a single opening
