11. SAKHTI [Stiffness, obduracy]

Synonyms: GHILZA, QASWA, BA’SA (BU’AS, BI’AS), KABDA

11.1 QASWA (قَسْوَۃ): QASWA (قَسْوَۃ) and GHILZA (غِلْظَ) have been discussed earlier. QASWA means being remorseless and ruthless from heart. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ 2:74

 ثُمَّ قَسَتۡ قُلُوبُكُم مِّنۢ بَعۡدِ ذَٲلِكَ فَهِىَ كَٱلۡحِجَارَةِ أَوۡ أَشَدُّ قَسۡوَةً۬‌ۚ

 Then after that your hearts hardened. They were as rocks, or even harder.

11.2 GHILZA (غِلْظَ): combination remorseless and severe. Refers to nature. Allah says in the Quran.

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:123

يَـٰٓأَيُّہَا ٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ قَـٰتِلُواْ ٱلَّذِينَ يَلُونَكُم مِّنَ ٱلۡڪُفَّارِ وَلۡيَجِدُواْ فِيكُمۡ غِلۡظَةً۬‌ۚ

 O you who believe! Fight those of the disbelievers who attack you, and let them find severity in you

11.3 BA’SA (باَْسَاء): BU’AS (بؤس) refers stiffness and rigor which may come from disputes or hunger. BA’S (بَاَْس) word is used in Quran for battles وَحِينَ ٱلۡبَأۡسِ‌ۗ (hardship, and in the time of conflict) (2:177), for punishment  إِذۡ جَآءَهُم بَأۡسُنَا (when Our calamity came upon them) (6:43), for calamity  بَأۡسً۬ا شَدِيدً۬ا (severe punishment) (18:2) all of them giving the meaning of grimness and hardship. BA’SAA (باَْسَاء) means a hardships or difficulties that can be financial or conflict related. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:42

وَلَقَدۡ أَرۡسَلۡنَآ إِلَىٰٓ أُمَمٍ۬ مِّن قَبۡلِكَ فَأَخَذۡنَـٰهُم بِٱلۡبَأۡسَآءِ وَٱلضَّرَّآءِ لَعَلَّهُمۡ يَتَضَرَّعُونَ

 We sent messengers to communities before you, and We afflicted them with suffering and hardship, that they may humble themselves.

11.4 KABADA (کَبَد): means liver. The meaning of word KABAD (کَبَد) consists of toughness and power. TAKABBAD (تَکَبَّد) and KAABAD (کَابد) means to bear hardship (dictionary Al Manjad). FI-KABAD (فِیْ کَبَد) is used as an idiom and represents human nature that when a man wishes for something, to fulfill the wish he endures pain, suffering and struggles for it, before it is rewarded some more wishes come through. Then to fulfill the other wishes he agrees to tolerate more pain and agony, and his whole life comes to end following the same pattern of struggle. Allah has described this fact in Quran as

Surah Al-Balad (The city): سورۃ البَلد         90:4

 لَقَدۡ خَلَقۡنَا ٱلۡإِنسَـٰنَ فِى كَبَدٍ

 We have created man into toil and struggle.

Summary:

11.1 QASWA: Ruthless from heart

11.2 GHILZA: Remorseless and vicious nature.

11.3 BA’SAA: Tenure of hardship.

11.4 KABAD: The nature of man to endure pain and struggle for life.
