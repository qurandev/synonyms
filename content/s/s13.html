13 SARDAR [Leader, Chief]

Synonyms: SAYYED, MALA’, RAHT, AI’MMA, NAQEEB

13.1 SAYYED (سَیِّد): SAAD (سَادَ) means honorable, wise, elder, head of a nation, to dominate due to grandeur and dignity (dictionary Al Manjad). SAWADA (سَوَادَ) means a large group, and SAYYED (سَیِّد) means the leader of a big group. A leader should be civilized therefore a noble person can also be called SAYYED (سَیِّد) and in the same manner master/lord or a husband can also be referred to as SAYYED (سَیِّد) (plural SAADAT (سَادَت) (33:67), SAADAAT (سَادَات)). Used in a positive sense. In the Quran

Suran Al-Ahzaab (The clans): سورۃ الأحزَاب 33:67

وَقَالُواْ رَبَّنَآ إِنَّآ أَطَعۡنَا سَادَتَنَا وَكُبَرَآءَنَا فَأَضَلُّونَا ٱلسَّبِيلَا۟

And they would say: "Our Lord! We obeyed our chiefs and our great ones, and they misled us as to the (right) Path

13.2 MALA’ (مَلَأ): MALA (مَلَا) means to fill, MALA’ (مَلَأ) means a group have a congregation for inviolability. Who fill the sight with apparent beauty and souls with horror. And their leaders gaze with pride move around causing terror. In commonly parlance MALA (ملا) means those government officials who due regal influence are imposed on people. Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:88

قَالَ ٱلۡمَلَأُ ٱلَّذِينَ ٱسۡتَكۡبَرُواْ مِن قَوۡمِهِۦ لَنُخۡرِجَنَّكَ يَـٰشُعَيۡبُ وَٱلَّذِينَ ءَامَنُواْ مَعَكَ مِن قَرۡيَتِنَآ

The leaders, the arrogant party among his people, said: "O Shu´aib! we shall certainly drive you out of our city

13.3 RAHT (رَھْط): Clan, group of people belonging to a tribe, who are less than 10 in number and have no women amongst them. The leader of this group is also called RAHT(رَھْط). Used in a negative sense. The shred of cloth which women place under their private parts during menstruation is also called RAHT (رَھْط) and hence the idiom “(ھُوَ اَذَلُّ مِنَ الَرَّھْط)” translation “more wretched then shred of menstruation”. When the word RAHT (رَھْط) refers to some number then its meaning is taken a people or individuals. Like ISHROON-A-RAHTAN (عِشْرُوْنَ رَھْطاً) means twenty people (dictionary Al Manjad). Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah An-Naml (The ant): سورۃ النَّمل 27:48

وَكَانَ فِى ٱلۡمَدِينَةِ تِسۡعَةُ رَهۡطٍ۬ يُفۡسِدُونَ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ وَلَا يُصۡلِحُونَ

There were in the city nine men of a family, who made mischief in the land, and would not reform

13.4 AI’MMAH (اَئِمَّۃ): plural of IMAM (اِمَام), a person, book or saying that is obeyed. Can be a human, a book or a quote can be righteous or wicked. In case of human mostly it’s a religious leader, or the one who leads the prayer. For instance

 Surah Yaseen (Yaseen): سورۃ ٰیٰس 36:12

 وَكُلَّ شَىۡءٍ أَحۡصَيۡنَـٰهُ فِىٓ إِمَامٍ۬ مُّبِينٍ۬

We have tallied all things in a Clear Record.

For leaders of guidance

Surah As-Sajdah (The Prostration): سورۃ السَّجدَہ 32:24

وَجَعَلۡنَا مِنۡہُمۡ أَٮِٕمَّةً۬ يَہۡدُونَ بِأَمۡرِنَا لَمَّا صَبَرُواْ‌ۖ

And We appointed leaders from among them, guiding by Our command

Leader of infidels

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:12

 فَقَـٰتِلُوٓاْ أَٮِٕمَّةَ ٱلۡڪُفۡرِ‌ۙ إِنَّهُمۡ لَآ أَيۡمَـٰنَ لَهُمۡ

Then fight the leaders of disbelief—they have no faith

13.5 NAQEEB (نقیب): NAQABA(نَقَبَ) means to drill through a wall, a subterraneous excavation, NAQABA-ANIL-AKHBAAR (نَقَبَ عَنِ الاَخْبَار) means to investigate the news, NA’QABA (نَاقَبَ) means to take pride in your actions, NA’QIB (نَاقِبْ) means someone who is aware of the condition of a nation, MUNQABAT (مُنْقَبَتْ) means a noble act. NAQEEB (نقیب) someone who is aware of the condition of a nation, a leader of a nation, chief, one whose accountable for a nation (dictionary Al Manjad). Used in a positive sense. Allah says in the Holy Quran.

Surah Al-Maeda (The table spread): سورۃ المَائدہ 5:12

 وَلَقَدۡ أَخَذَ ٱللَّهُ مِيثَـٰقَ بَنِىٓ إِسۡرَٲٓءِيلَ وَبَعَثۡنَا مِنۡهُمُ ٱثۡنَىۡ عَشَرَ نَقِيبً۬ا‌ۖ

God received a pledge from the Children of Israel, and we raised among them twelve chiefs.

Summary:

13.1 SAYYED: Leader of a big nation (positive sense)

13.2 MALA’: Officials imposed on people (negative sense)

13.3 RAHT: Small group of people from a tribe, mischievous group and its leader.

13.4 IMAM: Leader, one who is obeyed irrespective of him being good or bad.

13.5 NAQEEB: Guarantor, leader or guardian.
