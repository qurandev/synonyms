12 SAR UTHANA [raise head]

Synonyms: AQMAH, AQNAH, A’LA

12.1 AQMAH (اَقْمَحَ): when a camel lifts his head after finishing his drink looking down this condition is known as AQMAH-AL-BAEER (اَقْمَحَ البَعِیْر). And when a human is forced to lift his head and can only lower eyes to look down that is also known as AQMAH-AR-RUJULU-WAGHADDA-BASRUH (اَقْمَحَ الرَّجُلُ وَغَضَّ بَصَرُہُ) . Allah says in the Holy Quran

Surah Yaseen (Yaseen): سورۃ ٰیٰس 36:8

 إِنَّا جَعَلۡنَا فِىٓ أَعۡنَـٰقِهِمۡ أَغۡلَـٰلاً۬ فَهِىَ إِلَى ٱلۡأَذۡقَانِ فَهُم مُّقۡمَحُونَ

We have put yokes round their necks right up to their chins, so that their heads are forced up

12.2 AQNA’ (اَقْنَعَ): means to raise your hear or voice, to lift up. MUQNAE’ (مُقْنِع) means one who raises head to look. Allah says in the Quran.

Surah Ibraheem (Abraham): سورۃ ابراھیم 14:43

 مُهۡطِعِينَ مُقۡنِعِى رُءُوسِہِمۡ لَا يَرۡتَدُّ إِلَيۡہِمۡ طَرۡفُهُمۡ‌ۖ وَأَفۡـِٔدَتُہُمۡ هَوَآءٌ۬

 Their necks outstretched, their heads upraised, their gaze unblinking, their hearts void.

12.3 A’LA (عَلَا) YA’LAU (یَعْلَوْا) ULUWAN (عُلُوًّا): to rise, to raise your head (dictionary Al Manjad). Opposite of ULU (عُلْو) is SUFL(سَفْل). This is a common word but usually used as negative meaning. Both in material and meaning. On the contrary ALEEYA (عَلِیَ), YA’LI (یَعْلَی) conveys positive interpretation. Allah says in the Holy Quran.

Surah Al Qasas (The stories): سورۃ القَصَص 28:4

إِنَّ فِرۡعَوۡنَ عَلَا فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ

 Pharaoh exalted himself in the land

Summary:

12.1 AQMAH: Head raised yet gaze is low.

12.2 AQNAH: Head raised looking forward,

12.3 A’LA: Head risen in pride, obstinacy
