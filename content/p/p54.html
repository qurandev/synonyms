Giving birth -54

Synonyms are:

1) بَرَاَ

2) بَدَع

3) فَطَرَ

4) خَلَقَ

5) اَنْشَأَ

6) ذَرَاَ

1)baraa بَرَاَ: it means to bring a thing from inexistence or nothingness into being Munjid – منجد (Alfurooqul-lughwiyyah - الفروق اللغوية 113).to create something to invent something.

Allah says:

فَاقْتُلُوا أَنفُسَكُمْ ذَٰلِكُمْ خَيْرٌ لَّكُمْ عِندَ بَارِئِكُمْ

(Surah 2 al baqrah ,verse 54 )

Translation: and slay yourselves (the wrong-doers); that will be better for you in the sight of your Maker.

2) badaa بَدَع : creating for the first time. To create without any sample or following anything Munjid – منجد and badia بديع means the one who creates something unique or extraordinary .

Allah says

بَدِيعُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ

(SURAH 2 AL BAQARAH , VERSE 117)

Translation : The Originator of the heavens and the earth.

3) fatara فَطَرَ : to give the cuts and curves beautifully ayat “فِطْرَتَ اللَّهِ الَّتِي فَطَرَ النَّاسَ عَلَيْهَا”(surah 30 ar room,verse 30) is translated by sir usmani “Allah´s handiwork according to the pattern on which He has made mankind.”

Allah says

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ فَاطِرِ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ

(surah 35 faatir,verse number1)

Transaltion: Praise be to Allah, Who created (out of nothing) the heavens and the earth.

4)khlaaqaa خَلَقَ : this word can be taken in many ways

Such as

a)to make a diagram or to make a rough figure(appraise) before creating something Mufraddat – مفردات so creating something can be a job of brain and it can be attributed to someone other than ALLAH.

b)khlaqa خَلَقَ sumtime is also used in sense of ibdaa which means creating first time. In Quran

خَلْقَ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالأَرْضِ translated as”created the heavens and the earth “

And in the same way

“بَدِيعُ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضِ “ translated as “Originator of the heavens and the earth.”

in both cases creator is none other than Allah.

c)and the genral meaning of khalaqa is to create sumthing from already present material .means already the material is there and to create using the material .

ALLAH says

الَّذِي خَلَقَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍ وَاحِدَةٍ وَخَلَقَ مِنْهَا زَوْجَهَا وَبَثَّ مِنْهُمَا رِجَالًا كَثِيرًا وَنِسَاءً

(suran 4 an nisa verse number 1)

Transaltion : who created you from a single person, created, of like nature, His mate, and from them twain scattered (like seeds) countless men and women

In this case the word can be attributed to other then Allah as well .as Allah says to mosa (a.s)

وَإِذْ تَخْلُقُ مِنَ الطِّينِ كَهَيْئَةِ الطَّيْرِ

(suran 5 al maidah,verse number110)

Transaltion: and when you made out of the clay a figure like that of a bird.

And this ayah:

فَتَبَارَكَ اللَّهُ أَحْسَنُ الْخَالِقِينَ

(surah 23 al muminoon verse 14)

Transaltion: So blessed is Allah , the best of creators.

5)anshaa اَنْشَأَ :this word comes in two ways

( a) to give birth to something

(b)and then bought up that thing by breeding and feeding it Maqaayeesul-lughat - مقائيس اللغة-Munjid- منجد.this word is used in both senses separately and can also used at the same time giving both the meanings explained above in one sentence.

In terms of giving birth:

ALLAH SAYS:

وَهُوَ الَّذِي أَنشَأَكُم مِّن نَّفْسٍ وَاحِدَةٍ

(surah 6 al anaam verse 98)

Translation: It is He Who hath produced you from a single person.

IN TERMS OF BREEDING AND FEEDING(foster):

Allah says

أَوَمَن يُنَشَّأُ فِي الْحِلْيَةِ

(surah 43 al zukhraf,verse 18)

Transaltion: Is then one brought up among trinkets.

And if the word means IN BOTH TERMS GIVING BIRTH AND THEN BREEDING:

Allah says:

أَأَنتُمْ أَنشَأْتُمْ شَجَرَتَهَا أَمْ نَحْنُ الْمُنشِئُونَ

(surah 56 al waqiaa,verse 72)

Transaltion: Is it ye who grow the tree which feeds the fire, or do We grow it?

6)ZARAA ذَرَاَ

a)means to create or

b)to spread Maqaayeesul-lughat - مقائيس اللغة 

this word can be used in both ways, separately,portraying one meaning at the time or can be used with both meanings at the same time .

if thw word is used in terms of “creating” ALLAh says

وَلَقَدْ ذَرَأْنَا لِجَهَنَّمَ كَثِيرًا مِّنَ الْجِنِّ وَالْإِنسِ

(Surah 7 al aaraaf verse 179)

Transaltion: Many are the Jinns and men we have made for Hell.

And zaraa ذَرَاَ means to sow seeds in farms Munjid- منجد. and zaraa ذَرَء menas anything thing that can be sowed and farmed Maqaayeesul-lughat - مقائيس اللغة. The way how seeds are spread and are scattered, similarly human beings are also spread on earth and this word can be used in this sense as well.

Allah says

قُلْ هُوَ الَّذِي ذَرَأَكُمْ فِي الْأَرْضِ وَإِلَيْهِ تُحْشَرُونَ

(surah 67 al mulk verse 24)

Transaltion: Say, " It is He who has multiplied you throughout the earth, and to Him you will be gathered."

SUMMARY:

1)Braa : Without any material to bring sumthing into existence

2)badaa: without sample or following sumthing to create sumthing for the first time.

3)fataraa: by giving cuts and curves creating beautiful thing.

4)khlaqaa: to create a thing whose material is already available

5)anshaa: to give birth and foster.

6)zaraa: to create and spread .
