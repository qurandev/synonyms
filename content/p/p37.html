37. Phal پھل (Fruit)

Also commonly known as thamar ثمر , fakeh فاكِه , janni جَنى , ukul اُكل

1.      Thamar ثمر : means fruit. This is a commonly used word for a variation of fruits including fresh fruit, dry fruit and other type of fruit may also be encompassed in its meaning. The plural of this word is athmaar اثمار and thamraat ثمرات.

 

Allah Taa’la says:

 

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

 

(2:22)

 

فَأَخۡرَجَ بِهِۦ مِنَ ٱلثَّمَرَٲتِ رِزۡقً۬ا لَّكُمۡ‌ۖ

“[He] who made for you the earth a bed [spread out] and the sky a ceiling and sent down from the sky, rain and brought forth thereby fruits as provision for you. So do not attribute to Allah equals while you know [that there is nothing similar to Him].”

 

2.      Fakeha فَاكِهَة : All fruits with the exception of dates, grapes and pomegranate are called fakeha فَاكِهَة . And the plural for this is fawaakeh فواكه . However, to some scholars, the word fakeh فاكه stands for all fruits, including grapes, with the exception of dates and pomegranate. The following ayah of the Holy Quran validates the context of the aforementioned logic:

Surah Al Rahman (The Beneficent) سُوۡرَةُ الرَّحمٰن

(55:68)

فِيہِمَا فَـٰكِهَةٌ۬ وَنَخۡلٌ۬ وَرُمَّانٌ۬

“In both of them are fruit and palm trees and pomegranates.”

The above ayah verifies that the date-palm and pomegranate are a different category of fruits that do not include in the meaning of fakeh فاكه . In other contexts, however, fawaakeh فواكه is used for the meaning of dry fruits. Fakeha فَاكِهَ means to be lively, carrying a sprightly persona and the capability of amusement and joyfulness. Fakkaha فَكَّهَ means to provide or feed fruit to someone and it also means to bring joy and pleasure to someone by means of a delightful conversation or speech. It is because of this Ibn-e-Faris believes that fakeha فكهة is a fruit that brings relish and pleasure simultaneously.

3.      Janni جنى : Fresh fruit: meaning such a fruit that has reached its fruition, is ripe and ready to be picked, whether it is still hanging from a tree or has already been picked. Janni-ya جَنِىَ means to pick and choose a fruit or to pick it from a tree.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Rahman (The Beneficent) سُوۡرَةُ الرَّحمٰن

(55:54)

وَجَنَى ٱلۡجَنَّتَيۡنِ دَانٍ۬

“[They are] reclining on beds whose linings are of silk brocade, and the fruit of the two gardens is hanging low.”

4.      Ukul اُكُل : Signifies those particular parts of the plants and trees that could be included in the staple diet of a human being. In a contextual Arabic meaning, it signifies fruit, nutrition and bounteous sustenance.

As mentioned in the Holy Quran:

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:265)

كَمَثَلِ جَنَّةِۭ بِرَبۡوَةٍ أَصَابَهَا وَابِلٌ۬

“And the example of those who spend their wealth seeking means to the approval of Allah and assuring [reward for] themselves is like a garden on high ground which is hit by a downpour - so it yields its fruits in double. And [even] if it is not hit by a downpour, then a drizzle [is sufficient]. And Allah , of what you do, is Seeing.”

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Samar ثمر : A common word for fruit.

(2)   Fakeha فاكِه : All fruits withholding the exception of date palm and pomegranate; however in other contexts any delicious fruit or dry fruit.

(3)   Janni جَنى : The fresh and ripe lot of fruit that is either just picked or may still be hanging from a tree.

(4)   Ukul اُكل : The fruitful and feracious parts of a plant or a tree that would be used as nourishment for human beings.
