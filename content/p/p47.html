47. Peechhay پیچھے (Behind/Back)

Also known as ba’ad بعد , wara’aa ورا , khalf خلف  and  adbaar اَدبَار .

1.      Ba’ad بعد : It means back, after, afterwards. The meaning of this lies within the context of time. And its antonym is qabal قبل which means before.

In the Holy Quran:

Surah Al Baqarah (The Cow) سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

(2:56)

ثُمَّ بَعَثۡنَـٰكُم مِّنۢ بَعۡدِ مَوۡتِكُمۡ لَعَلَّڪُمۡ تَشۡكُرُونَ

“Then We revived you after your death that perhaps you would be grateful.”

2.      Adbaar اَدبَار : The back or behind of anything is called dubur دُبر , the antonym of which, is qubl قُبل. Its meaning is connected within the context of time; i.e. with the ending of something or right after it. With that in mind, dubur-as-salaat دبرالصلوة  would mean ‘after salaat(prayer)’ The plural is adbaar اَدبَار .

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Qaf (The Letter “Qaf”) سُوۡرَةُ قٓ

(50:40)

وَمِنَ ٱلَّيۡلِ فَسَبِّحۡهُ وَأَدۡبَـٰرَ ٱلسُّجُودِ

“And [in part] of the night exalt Him and after prostration.”

Adbaar اَدبَار means for something to back out and disappear. Therefore, in a different chapter in the Holy Quran,

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah At Tur (The Mount) سُوۡرَةُ الطُّور

(52:49)

وَمِنَ ٱلَّيۡلِ فَسَبِّحۡهُ وَإِدۡبَـٰرَ ٱلنُّجُومِ

“And in a part of the night exalt Him and after [the setting of] the stars.”

The eloquence of this verse is evident with the fact that the meaning and concept of adbaar اَدبَار are well comprehended.
