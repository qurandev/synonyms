Qarar Pakarna (to hold firmly, to settle)

1. قَرَّ (Qar’ra):to stay, stagnant, motionless or still and to be cold. The connection between the two is that there is a loss of energy or motion when something gets cold while movement requires energy or heat.

Allah SWT said in Surah Al-Ahzaab (33:33): وَقَرْنَفِىبُيُوتِكُنَّوَلاَتَبَرَّجْنَتَبَرُّجَالْجَـهِلِيَّةِالاٍّولَى “And stay in your houses, and do not display yourselves like (women used to in) the times of ignorance.”

2. اِسْتَويعَلي (Istawa Ala): to ride on an animal or vehicle and plant oneself firmly and confidently. The difference between قَرَّ and اِسْتَويعَلي is that later is used to indicate stillness when thing transforms from one state to another.

Allah SWT says in Surah Hud (11:44): وَاسْتَوَتْعَلَىالْجُودِىِّ “And the water was made to subside and the decree was fulfilled. And it (the boat of Noah AS which was sailing on the water) rested on (Mount) Judi.”

And then again in Surah Al-Muminoon (23:28): فَإِذَااسْتَوَيْتَأَنتَوَمَنمَّعَكَعَلَىالْفُلْكِ “(Noah AS) And when you have embarked on the ship, you and whoever is with you.”

Similarly He said in Surah Al-Araf (7:54): ثُمَّاسْتَوَىٰعَلَىالْعَرْشِ “then He settled (rose over) on the Throne.”

Summary:

1. قَرَّ: To be motionlessness in the first state

2. اِسْتَويعَلي: to be motionless or firm in the second state after transformation from the first.
