12- FAZOOL KHARCHI KARNA [Spending extravagantly]

 

Qur’an has referred to it by the words ASRAFA (اسرف) and BADHARA (بذر).

 

 

12.1 ASRAFA (اسرف): SARAFA (سرف) and ASRAFA (اسرف) means to go beyond moderate limits and its use is common for spending more than required in wealth or material things. And moderation limit is only for those things that are permitted and AMR MUBAH (امر مباح). Hence the meaning of extravagance is spending more than needed in permitted things. Allah Said:

Surat Al-'A`rāf (The Heights) - سورة الأعراف 7:31

وَّكُلُوْا وَاشْرَبُوْا وَلَا تُسْرِفُوْا  

Eat and drink, but be not excessive.

12.2 BADHARA (بذر):  BADHARA (بذر) and BAZAR (بزر) means seed and BADHARA (بذر) means to spread seeds, so the way seeds are spread on the ground, if wealth is also spread like this then it is called TABZEER (تبزیر). Hence whether the need is permitted or not, excessive spending of wealth is called TABZEER (تبزیر) and it is a worse action than ASRAFA (اسرف).

  Allah Said:

(Surat-Bani Isra’eel) (Children of Israel) سورة بنی اسراءیل 17:26-27

 اِنَّ الْمُبَذِّرِيْنَ كَانُوْٓا اِخْوَانَ الشَّيٰطِيْنِ وَلَا تُبَذِّرْ تَبْذِيْرًا    

 And do not spend wastefully. Indeed, the wasteful are brothers of the devils.

 

Summary:

ASRAAF is used for excessive spending on permitted things and TABZEER is used for excessive spending of wealth on both, permitted and  non-permitted things (nut commonly for non-permitted things).
