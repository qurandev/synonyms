44. Bikharna بکھرنا (Paraganda hona پراگندہ ہونا) To Scatter/disperse (To be disordered/confused)

Also known as ‘Inbaththa’ اِنبَثَّ, ‘Intashara’ اِنتَشَرَ, ‘Intathar’ اِنتَثَرَ (Nathar) نثر and ‘Infaddaa’  اِنفَضَّ– ‘Istataar’ اِستَطَارَ.

1.       Inbaththa اِنبَثَّ: ‘Baththaa’ بَثَّ means to disperse and spread far and wide. ‘Inbaththa’ اِنبَثَّ means for something to diverge into several directions and spread across at a distance. The intention of the object that is scattering is not valid in this case.

 

Allah Taa’la says:

 

Surah Al Waqia (The Inevitable) سُوۡرَةُ الواقِعَة

 

(56:5-6)

 

وَبُسَّتِ ٱلۡجِبَالُ بَسًّ۬ا (﻿٥﻿) فَكَانَتۡ هَبَآءً۬ مُّنۢبَثًّ۬ا

 

“And the mountains are broken down, crumbling - And become dust dispersing.”

 

2.      Intashara اِنتَشَرَ: ‘Nashar’ نشر means to spread and promulgate or promote. ‘Intashara’ اِنتَشَرَ means for people in a gathering or a procession to rise and leave, be on their own way.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Jumuah (The Congregation, Friday) سُوۡرَةُ الجُمُعَة

(62:10)

فَإِذَا قُضِيَتِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةُ فَٱنتَشِرُواْ فِى ٱلۡأَرۡضِ وَٱبۡتَغُواْ مِن فَضۡلِ ٱللَّهِ

“And when the prayer has been concluded, disperse within the land and seek from the bounty of Allah , and remember Allah often that you may succeed.”

3.      Infaddaa اِنفَضَّ: ‘Faddaa’ فَضَّ means to break something that it splits into separate pieces. ‘Infidaad’ اِنفضَاض means for something like glass to break and shatter into pieces. As though ‘Infaddaa’ اِنفَضَّ would mean for something have all its contents and constituents scatter and disperse.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Jumua (The Congregation, Friday) سُوۡرَةُ الجُمُعَة

(62:11)

وَإِذَا رَأَوۡاْ تِجَـٰرَةً أَوۡ لَهۡوًا ٱنفَضُّوٓاْ إِلَيۡہَا وَتَرَكُوكَ قَآٮِٕمً۬ا‌ۚ

“But when they saw a transaction or a diversion, [O Muhammad], they rushed to it and left you standing. Say, "What is with Allah is better than diversion and than a transaction, and Allah is the best of providers."

4.      Intathar اِنتَثَرَ: ‘Nathar’ نَثَرَ means abruption; for something to fall off and scatter. ‘Intithaar’ اِنتِثَار means the detachment or abruption of a nose. According to Ibn-e-Faris, ‘Nathar’ نَثَرَ means to let something deviate and fall down. In his own words, ‘Kalmat Taddulu Aala Ilqaai Shaiyein Muatfarriq’ كلمَة تَدُلُّ عَلَى اِلقَاءِ شَيٍ مُّتَفَّرِقٍ. ‘Nathar’ نَثَرَ is also used to express non-versified text. Thereby, ‘Intathar’ اِنتَثَرَ is only used for the non-living.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Infitar (The Cleaving) سُوۡرَةُ الانفِطار

(82:2)

وَإِذَا ٱلۡكَوَاكِبُ ٱنتَثَرَتۡ

“And when the stars fall, scattering,”

5.      Istataar اِستَطَارَ: ‘Taara’ طَارَ means a plumose or a feathery animal, or for something to fly in the air. ‘Taair’ طاَئر means a bird. ‘Istataar’ اِستَطَارَ means for something to rise and spread widely in the air and scatter.

Allah Taa’la says:

Surah Al Insan (The Man) سُوۡرَةُ الإنسَان

(76:7)

يُوفُونَ بِٱلنَّذۡرِ وَيَخَافُونَ يَوۡمً۬ا كَانَ شَرُّهُ ۥ مُسۡتَطِيرً۬ا

“They [are those who] fulfill [their] vows and fear a Day whose evil will be widespread.”

 

Conclusion:

(1)   Inbaththa اِنبَثَّ: To diverge and spread far and wide. (The purpose/intention does not count)

(2)   Intashara اِنتَشَرَ: The scattering of a gathering or a procession and be on their own way. (by their own will)

(3)   Infaddaa اِنفَضَّ: For something to disperse in a way that all the constituents are slowly separated and scattered.

(4)   Intathar اِنتَثَرَ: To scatter and fall. (used for nonliving objects)

(5)   Istataar اِستَطَارَ: For something to scatter and spread across in the space. (See ‘Uthana’ اٹھانا – To pick up)

 

For ‘Bikheyr-naa’ بکھیرنا (To spread/ scatter), see ‘Pheylanaa’ پھیلانا (To spread) and ‘Uraana’ اڑانا (To make fly)

For ‘Bigaar’ بگاڑ (distort/deform), see ‘Kharaabi’ خرابی (fault)

For ‘Bigaarna’ بگاڑنا (to ruin, to destroy), see ‘Fasaad kerna’ فساد کرنا (to corrupt)

For ‘Bulaana’ بلانا (To call), see ‘Pukaarna’ پکارنا (To call)
