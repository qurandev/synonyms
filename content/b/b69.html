69 – To be ill

 

The words سَقِيم, مَرِيض and حَرَضَ have been used in the holy Quran

 

69.1 سَقِيم: used to describe an illness, a syndrome and even disagreeable speech.  سقم is limited to bodily disorders.  Linguistic scholars consider the first stage of a disorder to be عليل (to feel queer or anomalous), if the person gets worse, he will be described as سَقِيم and if he gets even worse he is described as مَرِيض.  Allah (the exalted) says in the Quran,

 

Surat Aş-Şāffāt (Those who set the Ranks) - الصافات سورة

37: 88-89

 فَنَظَرَ نَظۡرَةً۬ فِى ٱلنُّجُومِ (﻿٨٨﻿) فَقَالَ إِنِّى سَقِيمٌ۬ (﻿٨٩﻿)

And he cast a look at the stars (88). And said, "Indeed, I am [about to be] ill."(89)

________________________________________

 

69.2 مَرِيض: with reference to illness it is the stage after سَقِيم.  الموت مرض is an illness which proves to be fatal. The word مرض is used for both diseases of the body and heart.  Allah (the exalted) states in the holy Quran:

 

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - البقرة سورة

2:10

 فِى قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌ۬ فَزَادَهُمُ ٱللَّهُ مَرَضً۬ا‌ (﻿١٠﻿)

In their hearts is disease, so Allah has increased their disease (10)

 

The diseases of the heart include vices related to ones character for example cowardice, miserliness, hypocrisy, disbelief, etc. The plural of مَرِيض is مَرْضَى (and its plural is مراض).

Allah (the exalted) states:

 

Surat At-Tawbah (The Repentance) - التوبة سورة

9:91

 لَّيۡسَ عَلَى ٱلضُّعَفَآءِ وَلَا عَلَى ٱلۡمَرۡضَىٰ وَلَا عَلَى ٱلَّذِينَ لَا يَجِدُونَ مَا يُنفِقُونَ حَرَجٌ (﻿٩١﻿)

There is not upon the weak or upon the ill or upon those who do not find anything to spend any discomfort (91)

________________________________________

 

69.3 حَرَضَ: to be stricken by a major illness and become feeble and weary as a result.  The expression ‘الحزن او المرض اَحرَضهُ’ means ‘a major illness or grief has worn him out’.  حرض refers to be near death because of being extremely feeble.  Allah (the exalted) says in the holy Quran:

 

Surat Yūsuf (Joseph) - يوسف سورة

12:85

 قَالُواْ تَٱللَّهِ تَفۡتَؤُاْ تَذۡڪُرُ يُوسُفَ حَتَّىٰ تَكُونَ حَرَضًا أَوۡ تَكُونَ مِنَ ٱلۡهَـٰلِكِينَ (﻿٨٥﻿)

They said, "By Allah , you will not cease remembering Joseph until you become fatally ill or become of those who perish."(85)

________________________________________

 

Summary:

1.      سَقِيم: only for bodily syndromes which are noncritical

2.      مَرِيض: used for physical, spiritual (related to the heart), grave and even noncritical illnesses

3.      حَرَضَ: a patient who is has become so weak that he is near death
