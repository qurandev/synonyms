13. Ehad/wada/iqrar (Promise. Threat, Appointment, Appointed place, appointed

         time. Covenant. Covenant of protection. Burden Treaty Knot, contracts, pledged)

 

Synonyms: Waad ,Waid,Mauidn,Miad,Aahida,Zimmatun,Asara,Meesaq,aqad

13.1: WAAD: to make promise, to make hopefull for something. It is one-sided. AKHLAF-AL-WAAD means not  fulfill the promise.

In Quran

Surat ibrahim (prophet ibrahim.) –

14:22

وَقَالَ الشَّيْطَانُ لَمَّا قُضِيَ الْأَمْرُ إِنَّ اللَّـهَ وَعَدَكُمْ وَعْدَ الْحَقِّ وَوَعَدْتُكُمْ فَأَخْلَفْتُكُمْ

 And Shaitan (Satan) will say when the matter has been decided: "Verily, Allah promised you a promise of truth. And I too promised you, but I betrayed you.

 

13.2: WAEED: A promise based on threat, warning

In quran

Surat Qaf

50:45

فَذَكِّرْ بِالْقُرْآنِ مَنْ يَخَافُ وَعِيدِ

But warn by the Qur'an, him who fears My Threat.

13.3: MAUIDT: MAUIDNN. All these words are the Masdar from waada yaeedu. Waada yaeedu waadn, waeedtun, maueedn, maueedtn. It means promise or to make promise. They are used in quran in such meanings.

13.4: MIIAD: it is ism-e-Zarf. In terms of time it means the time when the promise was made. And in terms of Place it means the place of promise.

In quran

Surat Al-Anfal

8:42

 وَلَوْ تَوَاعَدْتُمْ لَاخْتَلَفْتُمْ فِي الْمِيعَادِ ۙ

 If you had made an appointment [to meet], you would have missed the appointment.

Also

Surat Al-Imran

3:9

رَبَّنَا إِنَّكَ جَامِعُ النَّاسِ لِيَوْمٍ لَا رَيْبَ فِيهِ ۚإِنَّ اللَّـهَ لَا يُخْلِفُ الْمِيعَادَ

Our Lord, surely You will gather the people for a Day about which there is no doubt. Indeed, Allah does not fail in His promise.

13.5: AHEDA: to upkeep something. To accept.to protect it and complete it. Ahd means  loyality, friendship, Trust, responsibility,will and Wali ehad means successor of the king and according to some dictionaries ehad is two sided promise and according to some others, it is a conditional promise. Allah says in Quran

Surat Al-Isra

17:34

 وَأَوْفُوا بِالْعَهْدِ ۖ إِنَّ الْعَهْدَ كَانَ مَسْئُولًا

And fulfill [every] commitment. Indeed, the commitment is ever [that about which one will be] questioned.

Also

Surat Al-Baqarah

2:40

وَأَوْفُوا بِعَهْدِي أُوفِ بِعَهْدِكُمْ

fulfill My covenant [upon you] that I will fulfill your covenant [from Me]

 

13.6: ZIMMAH: it’s a type of promise i.e. a promise to be fulfilled. Also gives the meaning of protection. Responsibility of protection.Promise of giving shelter to someone. People of Zimma are those who are given protection by taking tax.

In quran

Surat At-Taubah

9:8

كَيْفَ وَإِنْ يَظْهَرُوا عَلَيْكُمْ لَا يَرْقُبُوا فِيكُمْ إِلًّا وَلَا ذِمَّةً ۚ

 How (can there be such a covenant with them) that when you are overpowered by them, they regard not the ties, either of kinship or of covenant with you?

 

13.7: ISR: Asara means to tie the knot in something and forcefully withholding it. Obstruct, heavy burden. Customs, Shariah limitations, imprisonment, Huge responsibility i.e. this word bears the meaning of ehad,habs, saql .

In Quran

Surat Al-Araf

7:157

وَيَضَعُ عَنْهُمْ إِصْرَهُمْ

he releases them from their heavy burdens

Also

Surat Al-Baqarah

2:286

رَبَّنَا وَلَا تَحْمِلْ عَلَيْنَا إِصْرًا كَمَا حَمَلْتَهُ عَلَى الَّذِينَ مِنْ قَبْلِنَا

our Lord! Lay not on us a burden like that which You did lay on those before us

13.8: MEESAQ: AL-wasaq  means a rope or chain which is used to tie something tightly and meesaq is that covenant  which is written down and the parties of covenant are obligated according to it.

In Quran

Surat An-Nisa

4:90

إِلَّا الَّذِينَ يَصِلُونَ إِلَىٰ قَوْمٍ بَيْنَكُمْ وَبَيْنَهُمْ مِيثَاقٌ

Except for those who take refuge with a people between yourselves and whom is a treaty

 

13.9: UQOOD: Aqada means to tie knot and uqdatn mean knot. Also used in materialistic sense.

In Quran

Surat Al-Falaq

113:4

وَمِنْ شَرِّ النَّفَّاثَاتِ فِي الْعُقَدِ

And from the evil of the witchcrafts when they blow in the knots.

Also like Intellectually aqdul baigh, aqdun nikah and aqdul ehad which means to strengthen the covenant like a knot. According to ibn-al-fars, aqd is more strong than meesaq.

In Quran

Surat Al-Maidah

5:1

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا أَوْفُوا بِالْعُقُودِ

O you who believe! Fulfill (your) obligations.

Sometimes Aqd is made strong with swearing, acknowledging and sometimes by writing.

In Quran

Surat An-Nisa

4:33

 وَالَّذِينَ عَقَدَتْ أَيْمَانُكُمْ فَآتُوهُمْ نَصِيبَهُمْ

And to those whom your oaths have bound [to you] - give them their share.

 

Summary:

 

1: WADA:  when someone is made hopeful then it is called promise. If this promise leads to a bad result then it is called WAEED. The time and place for the promise is called MEEAD. It is mostly one-sided.

2. AHAD: Such promise which is also protected and tried to be fulfilled. It is two-sided and conditional.

3. ZIMMAH: Derived from ehad. Promise to give Refuge.

4. ISR:The promise which is burdened with restriction. Either it is the obligations of customs or shariah.

5. MEESAQ: When promise is strengthen with repetition or by writing it down then it is called MEESAQ.

6.AQD: It justifies to be more stronger than MEESAQ and is sometimes made stronger by swearing.
