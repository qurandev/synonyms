19- Bunches

For bunches, 1. (talaa'), 2. (qutoof) and 3. (qinwaan) are used in the Quran.

1. Talaa':

The literal meaning of talaa' is to appear, to show, to be seen.

This word is frequently used for stars. It is also used for a cluster which begins to appear from the spathe (a

large sheathing bract enclosing the flower cluster)

of a date palm. (Munjad). Allah says in the Quran:

وَالنَّخْلَ بَاسِقَاتٍ لَهَا طَلْعٌ نَضِيدٌ

And tall date-palms, with ranged clusters; (50:10)

2. Qutoof:

This is the plural of qitaf, and refers to the ripe fruit ready for picking. Words of the Quran give an evidence of this, whereas fellows of Munjad translate this as 'picked fruit or cluster of grapes that is picked.' Allah says in the Quran:

فَهُوَ فِي عِيشَةٍ رَاضِيَةٍ فِي جَنَّةٍ عَالِيَةٍ قُطُوفُهَا دَانِيَةٌ

So he shall be in a life, well-pleasing.In a lofty Paradise, the fruits in bunches whereof will be low and near at hand. (69:21-23)

which makes it clear that the fruit is ripe, ready to be picked from the trees, as Allah used the word 'qutoof' in the ayah above.

3. Qinwaan:

This is the plural of 'qinu' and this word is associated only with the bunches of grapes and dates which is unripe yet. Allah says in the Quran:

وَمِنَ النَّخْلِ مِنْ طَلْعِهَا قِنْوَانٌ دَانِيَةٌ

And out of the date-palm and its spathe come forth clusters of dates hanging low and near (6:99)

Conclusion: The word talaa' is used for the fruit that appears from the spathe, the word qitaf is used for the ripe fruit ready to be picked from the tree and the stage in between refers to qinu. However, qinu is only used for the clusters of dates and grapes.
