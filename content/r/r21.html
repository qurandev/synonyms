21 RAIT [sand]

Synonyms: SARAAB, KATHEEB, AHQAAF, by Arabic dictionary the word used is RAML but this word is not used in Quran

21.1 SARAAB (سَرَاب): Every beverage is known as SHARAAB (شراب). Anything that seems like potable but is actually not a beverage is known as SARAAB (سَرَاب). Figuratively this word is used to describe anything that does not exist and is mostly used to describe a mirage (i.e. when the heat and light of sun makes sand appear like water to a distant observer). In the Quran

Surah Al-Noor (The light): سورۃ النور 24:39

كَسَرَابِۭ بِقِيعَةٍ۬ يَحۡسَبُهُ ٱلظَّمۡـَٔانُ مَآءً

their works are like a mirage in a desert. The thirsty assumes it is to be water.

21.2 KATHEEB (کَثِیِْب): means a sand dune. Allah says in Quran

Surah Al-Muzammil (The enshrouded one): سورۃ المُزمّل 73:14

كَانَتِ ٱلۡجِبَالُ كَثِيبً۬ا مَّهِيلاً

On the Day when the earth and the mountains tremble, and the mountains become heaps of sand.

21.3 AHQAAF (اَحْقَاف): (singular HAQF (حقف) which means a field of sand dunes) AHQAAF(اَحْقَاف) means a wide terrain consisting of thousands of sand dunes (Manjad). According to Ruba-e-Maskoon it refers to an area in Yemen that was the centre of the people of ‘Aad. In the Quran

Surah Al-Ahqaaf (The Dunes): سورۃ الاَحْقَاف 46:21

وَٱذۡكُرۡ أَخَا عَادٍ إِذۡ أَنذَرَ قَوۡمَهُ ۥ بِٱلۡأَحۡقَافِ

And mention the brother of Aad, as he warned his people at the dunes.

Summary:

21.1: SARAAB: a mirage i.e. field of sand appearing like water to a distant viewer.

21.2: KATHEEB: sand dune.

21.3: AHQAAF: wide terrain consisting of thousands of sand dunes.
