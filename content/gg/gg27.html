27 GHAIRNA

[To surround]

Synonyms: HAFFA(حف), AHATA(أحأط )(HAUT)( حوط), HASAR(حصر), HAAQA(حاق )(HAIQ)( حيق)

27.1 haffa(حف): means to surround something from both sides and haffah bakza(حفه بكذا) meaning to enclose and mahiffah(محفة) meaning carriage, a moving cart which is covered from shades from all sides. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Al-Kahf (The Cave) - سُوۡرَةُ الکهف

18:32

وَحَفَفۡنَـٰهُمَا بِنَخۡلٍ۬ وَجَعَلۡنَا بَيۡنَہُمَا زَرۡعً۬ا

Coin for them a similitude: Two men, unto one of whom We had assigned two gardens of grapes, and We had surrounded both with date-palms and had put between them tillage.

39:75

وَتَرَى ٱلۡمَلَـٰٓٮِٕڪَةَ حَآفِّينَ مِنۡ حَوۡلِ ٱلۡعَرۡشِ

And thou (O Muhammad) seest the angels thronging round the Throne, hymning the praises of their Lord. And they are judged aright. And it is said: Praise be to Allah, the Lord of the Worlds!

27.2 ahata (أحأط): meaning to enclose, to build four sided walls, to surround something in such a way that it is kept safe in such a way that it cannot get out by itself nor can someone else take something out of it. Just like a house is enclosed in four sided walls. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Yunus  - سُوۡرَةُ یُونس

10:22

وَجَآءَهُمُ ٱلۡمَوۡجُ مِن كُلِّ مَكَانٍ۬ وَظَنُّوٓاْ أَنَّہُمۡ أُحِيطَ بِهِمۡ‌ۙ

He it is Who maketh you to go on the land and the sea till, when ye are in the ships and they sail with them with a fair breeze and they are glad therein, a storm-wind reacheth them and the wave cometh unto them from every side and they deem that they are overwhelmed therein; (then) they cry unto Allah, making their faith pure for Him only: If Thou deliver us from this, we truly will be of the thankful.

________________

This word is also used in the literal meaning. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Al-Baqarah (The Cow) - سُوۡرَةُ البَقَرَة

2:81

بَلَىٰ مَن كَسَبَ سَيِّئَةً۬ وَأَحَـٰطَتۡ بِهِۦ خَطِيٓـَٔتُهُ ۥ فَأُوْلَـٰٓٮِٕكَ أَصۡحَـٰبُ ٱلنَّارِ‌ۖ

Nay, but whosoever hath done evil and his sin surroundeth him; such are rightful owners of the Fire; they will abide therein.

________________

27.3 ahsara(احصر): meaning to encircle, to surround something in such a way that it remains locked inside and cannot come outside, to detain someone and not let any kind of information, message or help to be passed inside. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Al-Tawba (Repentence) - سُوۡرَةُ التّوبَة

9:5

وَٱحۡصُرُوهُمۡ وَٱقۡعُدُواْ لَهُمۡ ڪُلَّ مَرۡصَدٍ۬‌ۚ

Then, when the sacred months have passed, slay the idolaters wherever ye find them, and take them (captive), and besiege them, and prepare for them each ambush. But if they repent and establish worship and pay the poor-due, then leave their way free. Lo! Allah is Forgiving, Merciful.

________________

27.4 haaqa(حاق): meaning something to befall on something and meaning that something befallen on something and surround it. Thus any type of enclosure which a person being enclosed feels on himself; some kind of difficulty, problem or calamity to befall on someone. And haiq(حيق) means reward or punishment for one’s actions; bad consequence of bad deeds thus this word is used in the negative sense. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Az-Zumar(The Troops) - سُوۡرَةُ الزُّمَر

39:48

وَحَاقَ بِهِم مَّا كَانُواْ بِهِۦ يَسۡتَہۡزِءُونَ

And the evils that they earned will appear unto them, and that whereat they used to scoff will surround them.

________________

At another location, it is stated:

Surat Fatir(The Angels) - سُوۡرَةُ فَاطِر

35:43

وَلَا يَحِيقُ ٱلۡمَكۡرُ ٱلسَّيِّئُ إِلَّا بِأَهۡلِهِۚۦ

(Shown in their) behaving arrogantly in the land and plotting evil; and the evil plot encloseth but the men who make it. Then, can they expect aught save the treatment of the folk of old? Thou wilt not find for Allah's way of treatment any substitute, nor wilt thou find for Allah's way of treatment aught of power to change.

________________

This part of the above mentioned ayat is also used as a proverb.

Summary:

1.HAFFA(حف):means only to surround.

2. AHATA(أحأط): means to surround in such a way as to protect.

3. AHSARA(احصر): to enclose and detain.

4. HAAQA(حاق): means some calamity to befall on someone.
