26 GHORRA

[Horse]

Synonyms: KHAIL(خيل), SAFINAAT(صفنت), JIYAD(جياد) JAUD(جود), A’ADIYAT(عاديات) ADAD(عدد)

26.1 khail(خيل): Khail(خيل )is unisex i.e. it can be used for any kind of horse either it is male or female. It is also used to refer to a group or herd of horses as ghanam(غنم) is used for a herd of goats. It is also used to refer to a horse rider. Allah states that:

________________

Surat An-Nahl(The Bee) - سُوۡرَةُ النّحل

16:8

وَٱلۡخَيۡلَ وَٱلۡبِغَالَ وَٱلۡحَمِيرَ لِتَرۡڪَبُوهَا وَزِينَةً۬‌ۚ

And horses and mules and asses (hath He created) that ye may ride them, and for ornament. And He createth that which ye know not.

________________

In this ayat, khail(خيل) is used as a unisex and in the following ayats, khail(خيل) is used for a horse rider or collectively for horse rider and his horse. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Al-Isra(Children of Israel) - سُوۡرَةُ بنیٓ اسرآئیل / الإسرَاء

17:64

وَأَجۡلِبۡ عَلَيۡہِم بِخَيۡلِكَ وَرَجِلِكَ

And excite any of them whom thou canst with thy voice, and urge thy horse and foot against them, and be a partner in their wealth and children, and promise them. Satan promiseth them only to deceive

 ________________

The basic meaning of khail(خيل) is having different kinds of thoughts in mind, and the word khayal(خيال) is derived from it. And alkhaila(الخيلاء) is used for such a sense of superiority which a horse rider feels as compared to a man on foot. Similarly, the word takhayyul(تخيل) is derived from it meaning to know about something from wisdom, to fly high.

26.2 saafinaat (صفنت): plural of saafin(صافن). Safana(صفن) meaning a horse standing on three legs in such a way that only the edge of the fourth foot is on the ground. And it refers to an active and energetic horse.

________________

26.3 jiyad (جياد): plural of jayyad(جيد). Jood(جود) means to have ampleness in something. And jawad(جواد) is used for a generous man or woman. Jaad al faras(جاد الفرس) means a lively and fast horse. The word jayyad(جيد) is also used to depict the finery of something. According to the people of lexical jurisprudence, jiyad(جياد) means a fast and fine horse. It is stated in the Quran:

________________

Surat Sad - سُوۡرَةُ صٓ

38:31

إِذۡ عُرِضَ عَلَيۡهِ بِٱلۡعَشِىِّ ٱلصَّـٰفِنَـٰتُ ٱلۡجِيَادُ

When there were shown to him at eventide lightfooted coursers

________________

26.4 a’adiyat (عاديات): plural of a’adiya(عادية) and a’adiya(عادية) is the feminine of a’adi(عادي) and a’adi(عادي) means that party which is ready for murder, chaos creating horses. A’d-al-fars(عد الفرس) meaning that the horse took a leap. Ta’ad-al-qaum(تعادي القوم) meaning people took part in a race. Thus a’adiyat(عاديات) means those horses going to war which take part in races. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Adiyat (The Chargers) - سُوۡرَةُ العَادیَات

100:1

وَٱلۡعَـٰدِيَـٰتِ ضَبۡحً۬ا

By the snorting coursers

________________

 

Summary:

1.KHAIL(خيل): unisex, its use is common. Used for horse, horse rider or collectively for both.

2. SAFINAT(صفنت): an active and energetic horse standing on three legs.

3. JIYAD(جياد): fast and fine type of horses.

4. A’ADIYAT(عاديات): those horses which take part in races and are trained for war.
