10 GUZARNA [to pass]

Synonyms: SABAQA, KHALA, SALAFA, MARRA, MADAA, INSALAKH

10.1 SABAQA (سَبَقَ): To leave behind others and move ahead. To advance. Thus one who passes ahead,  and always wanted be ahead of those who were behind. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Hashr (The exile): سورۃ الحَشر 59:10

 رَبَّنَا ٱغۡفِرۡ لَنَا وَلِإِخۡوَٲنِنَا ٱلَّذِينَ سَبَقُونَا بِٱلۡإِيمَـٰنِ

Our Lord, forgive us, and our brethren who preceded us in faith

When it is used for ‘passing’ then it means ‘passed earlier’ or  after passing this place a person  also went pass other things, or is still passing by some other things. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ta-Ha (Ta-ha): سورۃ طٰھٰ  20:99

كَذَٲلِكَ نَقُصُّ عَلَيۡكَ مِنۡ أَنۢبَآءِ مَا قَدۡ سَبَقَ‌ۚ

Thus We narrate to you reports of times gone by;

10.2 KHALA(خَلَا): Means empty. To separate one thing from other. To pass by something so that no one resists it. Used for both time and place. KHALAA (خَلّٰی) means to vacate, In the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:5

 فَخَلُّواْ سَبِيلَهُمۡ‌ۚ

then open the way for them

Means to give way, to get out of way and let someone pass through or leave the way and do not resist. KHALA’ (خَلَاء) is also an empty house and also the ‘space’ between the sky and earth. Thus KHALA (خلا) apart from passing someone also refers to empty space or moments. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:141

 تِلۡكَ أُمَّةٌ۬ قَدۡ خَلَتۡ‌ۖ لَهَا مَا كَسَبَتۡ

That was a community that has passed. To them is what they have earned, 

10.3 SALAFA (سَلَفَ): Means to move ahead, and SALAF-AL-QAWM (سَلَفَ الْقَوْم) means to be ahead of a nation or group (dictionary al Manjad). SALF (سَلْف) means an ancient, someone who has passed away (opposite KHALF(خَلْف)) and (plural ASLAAF(اَسْلاَف)) and ASLAAF (اَسْلاَف) means those pious elders who have passed away. SALAFI (سَلَفی) is a person who likes to connect with them. KHALAF means a loser or an unfit or unworthy successor.  Thus when SALAF (سَلَفَ) is referred to passing then it  depicts the forthcoming generations or similar events. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:23

وَأَن تَجۡمَعُواْ بَيۡنَ ٱلۡأُخۡتَيۡنِ إِلَّا مَا قَدۡ سَلَفَ‌ۗ

 And the wives of your genetic sons, and marrying two sisters simultaneously. Except what is past

10.4 MARRA (مَرَّ): To go past something, to walk past something. In the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:259

أَوۡ كَٱلَّذِى مَرَّ عَلَىٰ قَرۡيَةٍ۬

 Or like him who passed by a town

And ISRAMARR (اِسْتَمَرّ) means to keep on passing by, to be in a state of continuously passing by something (dictionary al Manjad). MAROOR-E-AYYAM (مَرُوْرِ اَیَّام) means passing of days. MARR (مَرّ) is also used in same meaning in Quran as ISTAMARR (اِسْتَمَرّ).  For instance

Surah Al-Araf (The heights): سورۃ الأعرَاف 7:189

 حَمَلَتۡ حَمۡلاً خَفِيفً۬ا فَمَرَّتۡ بِهِ

 she carries a light burden and continues therein.

In another place

Surah Al-Qamar (The moon): سورۃ القَمَر 54:2

 وَإِن يَرَوۡاْ ءَايَةً۬ يُعۡرِضُواْ وَيَقُولُواْ سِحۡرٌ۬ مُّسۡتَمِرٌّ۬

And if they see a miracle, they turn away and say, "Passing magic."

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:105

 يَمُرُّونَ عَلَيۡہَا

the earth do they pass by

Thus MARR (مَرّ) refers to passing by and also to ISTAMRAR (استمرار).

10.5 MAZAA (مَضٰی): Means to walk past, to leave. Opposite of MAZAA (مَضٰی) is ISTAQBALA (اِسْتَقْبَلَ) means to come from front, to approach from front. And MAZAA (مَضٰی) means to leave or move away from front. And MAAZI (ماضی) means past whose opposite is MUSTAQBIL (مستقبل) meaning future. Used for both novelty and grandee, for any kind or events. MAZAA-ALA-AL-AMAR (مَضٰی عَلیَ الْاَمْر) means continuity in work (dictionary al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Al-Anfal (The Spoils of War): سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنفَال 8:38

 وَإِن يَعُودُواْ فَقَدۡ مَضَتۡ سُنَّتُ ٱلۡأَوَّلِينَ

 But if they persist—the practice of the ancients has passed away.

In another place

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:60

 حَتَّىٰٓ أَبۡلُغَ مَجۡمَعَ ٱلۡبَحۡرَيۡنِ أَوۡ أَمۡضِىَ حُقُبً۬ا

until I reach the junction of the two seas, or I will pass epochs away."

10.6 INSALAKHA (اِنْسَلَخَ): SALAKHA (سَلَخَ) means to pull off sin or hide. And SALAKHA (سَلَخَ) and INSALAKH-ASH-SHAHAR (اِنْسَلَخَ الشَّھْر) means last dates of lunar month. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:5

فَإِذَا ٱنسَلَخَ ٱلۡأَشۡہُرُ ٱلۡحُرُمُ

When the Sacred Months have passed,

Summary:

10.1 SABAQA: To take over, also likes those whom left behind.

10.2 KHALA: Passing of something with account of empty space or time, and something coming in the place.

10.3 SALAFA: Passing of something with connection to the later one.

10.4 MARRA: Passing also with ISTAMARR or continuity, to go past something.

10.5 MADAA: To do something in the past time.

10.6 INSALAKH: Passing of a lunar month.
