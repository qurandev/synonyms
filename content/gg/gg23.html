23 GHARI

[Clock, time, period]

Synonyms: SA’A(ساعة), AANA(أناء), ZULF(زلف)

23.1 sa’a(ساعة): a specified period of time, instant (pl. saa’at(ساعات)). The people of Arab had divided the day and night into periods of 12 hours each and given them specific names whose details can be found in ‘din’ and ‘raat’. Nowadays as well, the complete period of day and night is divided into 24 hours. Thus, sa’a(ساعة) can also be used to depict one hour as Allah states that:

Surat Al-Ahqaf (The Dunes) - سُوۡرَةُ الاٴحقاف

46:35

لۡمۡ يَلۡبَثُوٓاْ إِلَّا سَاعَةً۬ مِّن نَّہَارِۭ‌ۚ

Then have patience (O Muhammad) even as the stout of heart among the messengers (of old) had patience, and seek not to hasten on the doom for them. On the day when they see that which they are promised (it will seem to them) as though they had tarried but an hour of daylight. A clear message. Shall any be destroyed save evil-living folk?

23.2 aana (أناء): aan(آن) meaning a period of time (pl. aana(أناء)) and anaa yaani(أني ياني) meaning the time of something has reached and it has reached its limit. Furthermore, anaa(أني) meaning a complete day or some part of it but in the Quran, the word aana(أناء) has been used thrice at 3:113, 20:13, 39:9 and in all these places, the word lail(ليل)(night) has been used with aana(أناء). It has never been used with day. Thus it can be understood that the word aana(أناء) is associated with the hours of night. Allah states that:

________________

Surat Al-E-Imran (Family of Imran) - سُوۡرَةُ آل عِمرَان

3:113

يَتۡلُونَ ءَايَـٰتِ ٱللَّهِ ءَانَآءَ ٱلَّيۡلِ وَهُمۡ يَسۡجُدُونَ

They are not all alike. Of the People of the Scripture there is a staunch community who recite the revelations of Allah in the night season, falling prostrate (before Him).

________________

Surat Hud  - سُوۡرَةُ هُود

11:114

أَقِمِ ٱلصَّلَوٰةَ طَرَفَىِ ٱلنَّہَارِ وَزُلَفً۬ا مِّنَ ٱلَّيۡلِ‌ۚ

Establish worship at the two ends of the day and in some watches of the night. Lo! good deeds annul ill-deeds. This is reminder for the mindful.

________________

Summary:

1.SA’A(ساعة): a particular time of day or night, era, day of judgment.

2. AANA(أناء): hours of night.

3. ZULF(زلف): initial hours or part of night.
