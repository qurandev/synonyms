16 GANADAGI – NAJASAT [filth, uncleanness]

Synonyms: TAFATHA, RIJZ, RUJZ, RIJS

16.1 TAFATHA (تَفَثَ): TAFATHA (تَفَثَ) means something getting unclean or filthy; QADAA TAFITH (قَضٰی تَفِثَ) is to remove this kind of filth (dictionary al Manjad). According to Abu- A’bedah it means to clip the nails, trimming the moustache, to put on a fragrance. And all the things that are forbidden for mehram except nikkah and the use of them like cleaning the body up, to clip the nails, to cut off the outgrown hair, to take a bath and wearing tidy clothes, putting on a fragrance. QADAA TAFITH (قَضٰی تَفِثَ) refers to all such things. Allah said in the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:29

 ثُمَّ لۡيَقۡضُواْ تَفَثَهُمۡ وَلۡيُوفُواْ نُذُورَهُمۡ وَلۡيَطَّوَّفُواْ بِٱلۡبَيۡتِ ٱلۡعَتِيقِ

Then let them end their untidiness and fulfill their vows and perform Tawaf around the ancient House."

16.2 RIJZ (رِجْز): RAJAZA (رَجَزَ) means to spread anguish, RAJAZA FULLAN (رَجَزَ فُلَانٌ) he read ‘agitated poems’. RIJZIA ASHAAR “ رجزیہ اشعار” are those poems that were sung or narrated during the war to motivate for the battle. In this phrase “عَزَابٌ مِّنْ رِجْزٍ اَلِیْم” RIJZ (رِجْز) means something that is causing agitation. And RIJZ (رِجْز) are those evil thoughts that come from devil, motivating and influence heart for evil or wicked stuff. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Anfal (The Spoils of War): سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنفَال 8:11

 إِذۡ يُغَشِّيكُمُ ٱلنُّعَاسَ أَمَنَةً۬ مِّنۡهُ وَيُنَزِّلُ عَلَيۡكُم مِّنَ ٱلسَّمَآءِ مَآءً۬ لِّيُطَهِّرَكُم بِهِۦ وَيُذۡهِبَ عَنكُمۡ رِجۡزَ ٱلشَّيۡطَـٰنِ

When He caused calm to fall on you as a security from Him and sent down upon you water from the cloud that He might thereby purify you, and take away from you the uncleanness of the Shaitan,

Here ‘رِجۡزَ ٱلشَّيۡطَـٰنِ ’ refers to those thoughts from devil that could have brooded as a result of failure in the battle of badar.

RUJZ (رُجْز) and RIJZ (رِجْز) are actually same (dictionary al Manjad). And have the difference between them is same as the difference between HAMAL (حَمْل) and HIMAL (حِمْل) or WAQR  (وَقْر) and WIQR (وِقْر)  (See BAUJH [burden] for details). And it refers to all the evil thoughts that reside in heart. They can be related to the sin of polytheism or some wicked thought. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Muddaththir (The Cloaked One): سورۃ المدَّثّر 74:4

وَثِيَابَكَ فَطَهِّرۡ وَٱلرُّجۡزَ فَٱهۡجُرۡ

And your clothing purify. And uncleanliness avoid.

16.3 RIJS (رِجْس): That filth or grim that is rendered impure by licit or Shairah laws (dictionary al Manjad). This word can be used to depict filth both in material form and semantically. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:145

 إِلَّآ أَن يَكُونَ مَيۡتَةً أَوۡ دَمً۬ا مَّسۡفُوحًا أَوۡ لَحۡمَ خِنزِيرٍ۬ فَإِنَّهُ ۥ رِجۡسٌ

except carrion, or spilled blood, or the flesh of swine—because it is impure—

Another place in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:125

 وَأَمَّا ٱلَّذِينَ فِى قُلُوبِهِم مَّرَضٌ۬ فَزَادَتۡہُمۡ رِجۡسًا إِلَىٰ رِجۡسِهِمۡ

And as for those in whose hearts is a disease, it adds uncleanness to their uncleanness

Here RIJS (رِجْس) means the filth or uncleanness of infidelity and polytheism. And here the words depicts its semantic meaning. (Also see ‘impure’)

Summary:

16.1 TAFATHA: Filth of body.

16.2 RIJZ: Thoughts of agitation from devil

16.3 RIJS: Filth of sordid and forbidden things.
