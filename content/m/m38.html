38 MILNA [meet, mix]

Synonyms: LAQIA, LAHAQA, WALIA, ASNA, IKHTALATA, TAJAWAR, RATAQA, TAHYYAZ, ISHTAMMALA

38.1 LAQIA (لَقَیَ): Two things meeting each other from front. To visit. To meet coming from front. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:14

وَإِذَا لَقُوا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا قَالُوا آمَنَّا

And when they meet those who believe, they say, "We believe";

38.2  LAHAQA (لَحَقَ): Something meeting a similar thing, LAHIQA (لَاحِقَ) a thing that meets the one before. The fruit that comes after the first time (dictionary al Manjad). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ale- Imran (The family of Imran): سورۃ آل عِمْرن 3:170

وَيَسْتَبْشِرُونَ بِالَّذِينَ لَمْ يَلْحَقُوا بِهِم مِّنْ خَلْفِهِمْ

 they receive good tidings about those [to be martyred] after them who have not yet joined them -

Surah Al-Jumah (The Congregation, Friday) - سورة الجمعة 62:3

وَآخَرِينَ مِنْهُمْ لَمَّا يَلْحَقُوا بِهِمْ

And [to] others of them who have not yet joined them. And He is the Exalted in Might, the Wise.

Page 822

38.3  WALIA (وَلِیَ):  WALIA (وَلِیَ) and WALA-YAL-WALIAN (وَلَی یَلی وَلْیاً)means to be near or close, contiguous; to follow someone without distance (dictionary al Manjad). To seem like meeting because of closeness. This word is both used for place and time. To be close due to blood. Closeness due to friendship or religion. Following is the example with respect to place

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:123

يَا أَيُّهَا الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا قَاتِلُوا الَّذِينَ يَلُونَكُم مِّنَ الْكُفَّارِ

O you who have believed, fight those adjacent to you of the disbelievers

It means to fight those infidels who live near to you first and if they turn to Muslim and accept the religion then fight those who live next to them and further on. In case of time this word is perhaps not used in Quran. But according to the hadith of Prophet Muhammad (S.A.A.W)

خَیْرُ الْقُرُوْنِ قَرْنِیْ ثُمَّ الَّذِیْنَ یَلُوْنَھُمْ ثُمَّ الَّذِیْنَ یَلُوْنَھُمْ۔

The best of mankind is my generation, then those that follow them and then those that follow them.

And it means the best time are that of Prophet Muhammad (S.A.A.W) and then of his companions (R.A.A) and then his followers.

 

 

38.4 ASNA (اَصْنٰی): ASNA-AN-NAKHAL (اَصْنٰی النخل) means sprouting of two date palms from one root. SINW (صِنْو) is the depth between two mountains and where the water flows. And if two date palms sprout from a single root then each of them is the SINW (صِنْو) of other. In the same way real brothers are also SINW (صِنْو) of one another. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Rad (The Thunder): سُوۡرَةُ الرّعد13:4

وَنَخِيلٌ صِنْوَانٌ وَغَيْرُ صِنْوَانٍ يُسْقَىٰ بِمَاءٍ وَاحِدٍ

 and date-palms, growing out two or three from a single stem root,

38.5 IKHTALATA (اِخْتَلَطَ): Means mixing together of two things, (opposite KHALAS (خَلَصَ)) intermixed or intermingled. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Kahf (The Cave): سورۃ الکھف 18:45

كَمَاءٍ أَنزَلْنَاهُ مِنَ السَّمَاءِ فَاخْتَلَطَ بِهِ نَبَاتُ الْأَرْضِ

We send down from the sky, and the vegetation of the earth mingles with it

38.6 TAJAWAR (تَجَاوَرَ): JAR (جار) means neighbor, JAWARA (جَاوَرَ) and TAJAWARA (تَجَاوَرَ) something being contiguous due to the small distance between. To live next to each other. To live in the same neighborhood (dictionary al Manjad). To be together, to live in the neighborhood. In the Quran

Surah Al-Rad (The Thunder): سُوۡرَةُ الرّعد13:4

وَفِي الْأَرْضِ قِطَعٌ مُّتَجَاوِرَاتٌ

And within the land are neighboring plots

38.7 RATAQA (رَتَقَ): Means joining together of two or more things, to be glued together (dictionary al Manjad). RATQA’ (رَتقَاء) is that woman, the edge of whose private parts are joined together so that she cannot be copulated. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Anbiya (the prophets): سورۃ الانَبیاء 21:30

أَوَلَمْ يَرَ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا أَنَّ السَّمَاوَاتِ وَالْأَرْضَ كَانَتَا رَتْقًا فَفَتَقْنَاهُمَا

Have those who disbelieved not considered that the heavens and the earth were a joined entity, and We separated them

Page 823

38.8 TAHYYAZ (تَحَیَّزَ): From the dictionary of opposites, Means to get separated and meet again.

38.9 ISHTAMALA (اِشْتَمَلَ): ASH-SHAML (اَلشَّمْل) means a combined task. Contingent matter. JAMAA’-ALLAHU-SHAMLAHUM ( جَمَعَ اللہُ شَمْلَھُمْ) means Allah merged their dispersed tasks. FARRAQA-ALLAHU-SHAMLAHUM (فَرَّقَ اللہُ شَمْلَھُمْ) means Allah broke up their assemblage. SHAMILA (شَمِلَ) means to cover with a sheet. SHAMALA (شَمَلَ) and SHAMILA (شَمِلَ) to include everyone. To be usual (dictionary al Manjad). ISHTAMALA (اِشْتَمَلَ) means to combine the dispersed and separated ingredients to the real thing. To combine. In the Quran

Surah Al-Ana’am (The Cattle): سورۃ الانعام 6:143

أَمَّا اشْتَمَلَتْ عَلَيْهِ أَرْحَامُ الْأُنثَيَيْنِ

or (the young) which the wombs of the two females enclose?

Summary:

38.1 LAQIA: Two things meeting together from front.

38.2 LAHAQA: Something meeting a similar older thing.

38.3 WALIA: Two living things meeting each other with respect to time or place.

38.4 ASNA: Those combined things whose roots are same.

38.5 IKHTALATA: To mix up, to intermix.

38.6 TAJAWAR: Two things meeting each other being near.

38.7 RATAQA: Meet and get joined. Sealed opening.

38.8 TAHYYAZ: To get separated and meet again.

38.9 ISHTAMMALA: Dispersed items of something combining in assemblage. To get included.
