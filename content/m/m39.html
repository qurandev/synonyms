39 MILANA [to mix, to arrange to meet]

Synonyms: KHALATA, ALHAQA, WASALA, ALLAFA, LABASA, RAKKABA, DAMMA, MASHAJA, MARAJA, SHABA, DAGHATHA

39.1 KHALATA (خَلَطَ): (Opposite KHALASA (خَلَصَ)) to mix two or more things. To intermix or intermingle. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Tawbah (The repentance): سورۃ التوبھ 9:102

وَآخَرُونَ اعْتَرَفُوا بِذُنُوبِهِمْ خَلَطُوا عَمَلًا صَالِحًا وَآخَرَ سَيِّئًا

Others have confessed their sins, having mixed good deeds with bad deeds

Page 824

39.2 ALHAQA (اَلْحَقَ): Means to mix from back. In the Quran

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:101

تَوَفَّنِي مُسْلِمًا وَأَلْحِقْنِي بِالصَّالِحِينَ

Receive my soul in submission, and unite me with the righteous.”

39.3 WASALA (وَصَلَ): Means mixing of two things so that they become one, WASAAL (وصال) (Opposite HIJRAAN (ھجران)) and ATSAAL (اتصال) (Opposite ANFSAAL(انفصال)) to bring together, to join two things. This word is used both semantically and materially. Materially it means to reach something. And semantically to make up connections and to bound up with. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:27

وَيَقْطَعُونَ مَا أَمَرَ اللَّهُ بِهِ أَن يُوصَلَ

and sever that which Allah ordered to be joined,

39.4 ALLAFA (اَلَّفَ): Means to mix together the dispersed elements of a single thing, to recollect some dispersed things systematically. TALEEF-E-QULOOB (تالیفِ قلوب) means to remove the differences from heart and bring them together and inculcate love. ULFAT (اُلْفَت) means loving from heart. TALEEF (تألیف) is that idiom whose parts are put together by taking care their priorities and delays. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Anfal (The Spoils of War): سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنفَال 8:63

وَأَلَّفَ بَيْنَ قُلُوبِهِمْ ۚ لَوْ أَنفَقْتَ مَا فِي الْأَرْضِ جَمِيعًا مَّا أَلَّفْتَ بَيْنَ قُلُوبِهِمْ

And He has brought their hearts together (Literally: joined "between" their hearts). If you had expended whatever is in the earth altogether, in no way could you have brought their hearts together;

39.5 LABASA (لَبَسَ): YALBASU (یَلْبَسُ) means to wear clothes, to cover the body, LABSA-YALBISU (لَبَسَ یَلْبِسُ) means to mix one thing in another making the original thing suspicious and doubtful. TALBEES (تلبیس) means to hide the truth by displaying false and untrue things (dictionary al Manjad), and LABS (لبس) comes in the meaning of suspicion (see SHAKK O SHUBA [suspicion]).  LABASA (لَبَسَ) is only used in negation and only in talks. Like mixing up truth and lies, virtue and vice. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:42

وَلَا تَلْبِسُوا الْحَقَّ بِالْبَاطِلِ

 And do not mix truth with falsehood

39.6 RAKKABA (رَکَّبَ): RAKABA (رَکَبَ) means to mount, something mounting another thing. TARAKABA (تَرَاکَبَ) means to put one thing on another and mix them. RAKKABA (رَکَّبَ) means to mix together one thing with other, then mix in the third thing, then the fourth and similarly mixing them together and making them one. TARKEEB (ترکیب) is a famous word. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Infitaar (The Cleaving) - سورة الإنفطار 82:7 - 8

الَّذِي خَلَقَكَ فَسَوَّاكَ فَعَدَلَكَ فِي أَيِّ صُورَةٍ مَّا شَاءَ رَكَّبَكَ

Who created you, fashioned you perfectly, and gave you due proportion; In whatever form He willed, He put you together.

Page 825

39.7 DAMMA (ضَمَّ): Joining one part of a thing to its other part. To include; DAMEEMAH-AL-KITAB (ضمیمۃ الکتاب) is a famous word. In the Quran

Surah Ta-Ha (Ta-ha): سورۃ طٰھٰ  20:22

وَاضْمُمْ يَدَكَ إِلَىٰ جَنَاحِكَ تَخْرُجْ بَيْضَاءَ

And press your hand to your side, it shall come out white

39.8 MASHAJA (مَشَجَ): To mix together, to intermix, MASHAJ (مشج) and MASHEEJ (مشیج) means mixed, (plural AMSHAAJ (امشاج)) (dictionary al Manjad). To mix together  things of the same gender and making them one.

Surah Al-Insan (The Man) - سورة الانسان 76:2

إِنَّا خَلَقْنَا الْإِنسَانَ مِن نُّطْفَةٍ أَمْشَاجٍ نَّبْتَلِيهِ

Verily, We have created man from Nutfah drops of mixed semen (discharge of man and woman),

39.9 MAZAJA (مَزَجَ): MAZAJA-ASH-SHARAAB (مَزَجَ الشراب) to mix something in a drink, the thing that is mixed into the liquid is MIZJ (مِزْج) and MIZAAJ (مِزَاج) (dictionary al Manjad). Medically when the different parts of a body are mixed together this condition is known as MIZAAJ (مِزَاج), condition or state. In the Quran

Surah Al-Insan (The Man) - سورة الانسان 76:17

وَيُسْقَوْنَ فِيهَا كَأْسًا كَانَ مِزَاجُهَا زَنجَبِيلًا

And they will be given to drink there a cup (of wine) mixed with Zanjabil (ginger, etc.),

39.10 MARAJA (مَرَجَ): To things mixing together so that their individual properties remain in contact. GHUSNUN-MAREEJ (غَصْنٌ مَرِیْجٌ) means an intertwined branch of tree. AMAR-MAREEJ (امرمریج)means a complicated and difficult matter in which two or more opinions mix. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Ar-Rahman (The Beneficent): سُوۡرَةُ الرَّحمٰن 55:19

مَرَجَ الْبَحْرَيْنِ يَلْتَقِيَانِ

He has let loosed the two seas (the salt water and the sweet) meeting together.

39.11 SHABA (شَابَ): Means to mix, to trick someone, defalcation (dictionary al Manjad). (SHOUB (شوب)) or honey or any eatable that is mixed into other things. Honey is called SHOUB (شوب) because it is usually mixed into drinks. Similarly SHA’IBA (شائبه) is a popular word which means to mixed or counterfeit. In the Quran

Surah As-Saaffat (The Aligners): سُوۡرَةُ الصَّافات37:67

ثُمَّ إِنَّ لَهُمْ عَلَيْهَا لَشَوْبًا مِّنْ حَمِيمٍ

Then indeed, they will have after it a mixture of scalding water.

39.12 DAGHATHA (ضَغَثَ): DAGHATHA-AL-HADEETH (ضَغَثَ الحدیث) means to mix a talk, to intermix and DAGHATHA-MIN-AL-KHAIR-WAL-AMR (ضَغَثَ مِن الخیر و الامر) means a mixed matter which does not have base or is untrue. ADGHATH-AR-RO’IA (اَضْغَثَ الرُّؤیَا) means to describe a dream with suspicion. ADGHATHU-IHLAM (اَضْغَاثُ اَحْلام) means a nightmare. Mixed dreams which cannot be interpreted (dictionary al Manjad). In the Quran

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:44

قَالُوا أَضْغَاثُ أَحْلَامٍ ۖ وَمَا نَحْنُ بِتَأْوِيلِ الْأَحْلَامِ بِعَالِمِينَ

They said, "[It is but] a mixture of false dreams, and we are not learned in the interpretation of dreams."

Page 826

Summary:

39.1 KHALATA: To intermix different things.

39.2 ALHAQA: To mix from the back.

39.3 WASALA: To mix together and join.

39.4 ALLAFA: To unite dispersed elements.

39.5 LABASA: To mix one thing with another in order to cheat.

39.6 RAKKABA: To mix one thing in another, then mix another.

39.7 DAMMA: To join together to parts of a same thing.

39.8 MASHAJA: Mixing together to similar things,

39.9 MADAJA: To mix something in a drink.

39.10 MARAJA: To mix two similar things so that their individual properties remain intact.

39.11 SHAUB: To mix an eatable into other and consume them.

39.12 DAGHATHA: To mix a conversation, mixed conversation.
