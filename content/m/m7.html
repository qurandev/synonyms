7 MITANA [to erase, to remove]

Synonyms: TAMASA, MAHAQA, MAHA, NASAKHA

7.1 TAMASA (طَمَسَ): To erase something by rubbing or scraping leaving some effects; to obliterate. TAMASA (طَمَسَ) word is mostly used for eyes, stars and face. For eyes it refers to losing sight, for stars it refers to losing their light or dimming. And for face it means it gets mutilated and gets ugly. Allah says in the Quran

Surah An-Nisa (The women): سورۃ النِّسا 4:47

آمِنُوا بِمَا نَزَّلْنَا مُصَدِّقًا لِّمَا مَعَكُم مِّن قَبْلِ أَن نَّطْمِسَ وُجُوهًا

Believe in what We have sent down [to Muhammad], confirming that which is with you, before We obliterate faces and turn them toward their backs or curse them

In another place in Holy Quran:

Surah Al-Mursalat (The Emissaries): سُوۡرَةُ المُرسَلات 77:8

فَإِذَا النُّجُومُ طُمِسَتْ

Then when the stars become dim;

7.2 MAHAQA (مَحَقَ): To weaken someone or impair him, invalidate, or making him helpless or destroy. And MAHAQ (محق) means to lessen or reduce and its opposite is RABU (ربو) or RABI (ربی) which means to nourish and raise. To nurture and bring up. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:276

يَمْحَقُ اللَّهُ الرِّبَا وَيُرْبِي الصَّدَقَاتِ

Allah destroys interest and gives increase for charities.

7.3 MAHA (محو): To erase something leaving no trace; to abolish. ‘مَحَتِ الرّیْحُ السَّحَابَ’ wind blew away the clouds or obliterated the clouds. And its opposite is ATHBATA (اَثْبَتَ) means to uphold. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Rad (The Thunder): سُوۡرَةُ الرّعد13:39

يَمْحُو اللَّهُ مَا يَشَاءُ وَيُثْبِتُ

 God abolishes whatever He wills, and He affirms.

7.4 NASAKHA (نَسَخَ): Means to nullify, to invalidate, to mutilate or erase (dictionary al Manjad). To remove one thing and putting another instead. The thing that was removed or erased before is MANSUKH (منسوخ) and one that is brought in is NASIKH (ناسخ). Sometimes this word is used for only erasing and nullifying. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Hajj (The Pilgrimage): سُوۡرَةُ الحَجّ 22:52

فَيَنسَخُ اللَّهُ مَا يُلْقِي الشَّيْطَانُ ثُمَّ يُحْكِمُ اللَّهُ آيَاتِهِ

 But God nullifies what Satan interjects, and God affirms His revelations.

 

And sometimes it is used for abrogating something and replacing it. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:106

مَا نَنسَخْ مِنْ آيَةٍ أَوْ نُنسِهَا نَأْتِ بِخَيْرٍ مِّنْهَا أَوْ مِثْلِهَا

We do not abrogate a verse or cause it to be forgotten except that We bring forth [one] better than it or similar to it

Synonyms:

7.1 TAMASA: To erase so as to leave behind a trace.

7.2 MAHAQA: To weaken someone or making him helpless.

7.3 MAHA: Erase something leaving no trace.

7.4 NASAKHA: To remove one thing and putting another instead.
