12 MOHABBAT – MOHABBAT KERNA [Love, to love]

Synonyms: HABBA, MAHABBATAH, WADDA WUDDA, ALLAFA, SHAGHAFA, URUBAN

12.1 HABBA (حَبَّ) and MAHABBATAH (مَحَبَّةً): Means to aim for something by thinking of it as good. To desire for and use wisdom in order to get, regardless of it being woman or child, or wealth and gold. HABBA (حَبَّ) and HABBATHA (حَبَّة) means a grain (i.e. wheat grain or barley). And HABBAT-UL-QALB (حَبَّةُ القلب ) means pleasure of heart. In the Quran

Surah Saad (Saad): سورۃ ص 38:32

فَقَالَ إِنِّي أَحْبَبْتُ حُبَّ الْخَيْرِ عَن ذِكْرِ رَبِّي حَتَّىٰ تَوَارَتْ بِالْحِجَابِ

He said, “I have preferred the love of niceties to the remembrance of my Lord—until it disappeared behind the veil.

Another place in the Quran

Surah Ta-Ha (Ta-ha): سورۃ طٰھٰ  20:39

وَأَلْقَيْتُ عَلَيْكَ مَحَبَّةً مِّنِّي

 And I have bestowed upon you love from Me,

12.2 WADDA (وَدَّ): To wish for something, to be friend, to love (dictionary al Manjad). This word sometimes only refers to wishing and desiring. For instance in Quran

Surah Al-Hijr (The Rock): سورۃ الحِجر 15:6

رُّبَمَا يَوَدُّ الَّذِينَ كَفَرُوا لَوْ كَانُوا مُسْلِمِينَ

Perhaps those who disbelieve will wish that they had been Muslims.

And when it is used for referring to love, then it means extreme love. MAUDAH (مؤدۃ) means mutual love and care. WADOOD (وَدُوْد) means someone who is loving, WUDD (وُدّ) refers to love and caring

Surah Maryam (Mary): سُوۡرَةُ مَریَم 19:96

إِنَّ الَّذِينَ آمَنُوا وَعَمِلُوا الصَّالِحَاتِ سَيَجْعَلُ لَهُمُ الرَّحْمَٰنُ وُدًّا

On those who believe and work deeds of righteousness, will (Allah) Most Gracious bestow love.

In another place in Quran

Surah Hud (Hud): سورۃ ھود 11:90

ۚ إِنَّ رَبِّي رَحِيمٌ وَدُودٌ

My Lord is Merciful and Loving.

Thus the word WADDA (وَدَّ) when refers to love then it is prolific to HABB (حَبَّ)

12.3 ALLAFA (اَلَّفَ): To combine separated parts of something, from this word comes the word TALEEF (تالیف) which means to develop bonds and attachment. ULFAT (اُلْفَت)means love that is due to attachment. Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Anfal (The Spoils of War): سُوۡرَةُ الاٴنفَال 8:63

لَوْ أَنفَقْتَ مَا فِي الْأَرْضِ جَمِيعًا مَّا أَلَّفْتَ بَيْنَ قُلُوبِهِمْ وَلَٰكِنَّ اللَّهَ أَلَّفَ بَيْنَهُمْ

 If you had spent all that is in the earth, you could not have brought their hearts together;

12.4 SHAGHAFA (شَغَفَ): SHAGHAFAT-UL-QALB (شَغَفَۃُ ٓالْقَلْْب) means the interior of heart. Meaning membrane of heart. SHAGHAFA (شَغَفَ) means to hurt the membrane of heart. SHAGHAFA HUBBAN (شَغَفَ حُبًّا) means love penetrating deep in heart. To be insanely or deeply in love. In the Quran

Surah Yusuf (Joseph): سورۃ یُوسُف 12:30

قَدْ شَغَفَهَا حُبًّا ۖ

She is deeply in love with him

12.5 URUBAN (عُرُبًا): (singular AROOB (عَرُوب)) AROOB (عَرُوب) means a woman who loves her husband or a lively woman. In the Quran

Surah Al-Waqiah (The Inevitable): سُوۡرَةُ الواقِعَة 56:36

فَجَعَلْنَاهُنَّ أَبْكَارًا عُرُبًا أَتْرَابًا

Then We have made them virgins, Loving (their husbands only), equal in age.

Summary:

12.1 HABBA: Aim and desire for something

12.2 WADDA: To HABB to love dearly, to love.

12.3 ALLAFA: Love due to bonds and attachment,

12.4 SHAGHAFA HUBBA: Extreme love,

12.5 URUBAN: A loving wife, a woman who loves her husband
