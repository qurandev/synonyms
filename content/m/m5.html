5 MAAN [Mother]

Synonyms: WALIDAH, UMM

5.1 WALIDAH (وَالِدَۃ): The woman who gives birth to a child, this word is popular and has a specific use. When both the mother and father are mentioned then word WALDAIN (والدین) is used. (Plural WALIDAAT (والدات)). Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al-Baqarah (The cow): سورۃ البَقَرَۃ  2:233

وَالْوَالِدَاتُ يُرْضِعْنَ أَوْلَادَهُنَّ حَوْلَيْنِ كَامِلَيْنِ

Mothers may nurse their infants for two whole years,

5.2 UMM (اُمّ): This word refers to a larger set of meanings. UMM (اُمّ) is the real mother and also anything that is cause of birthing, or tutoring and nurturing of another thing. This word is used in the following meanings

1. As the real mother, Allah says in the Quran

Surah Al Qasas (The stories): سورۃ القَصَص 28:7

وَأَوْحَيْنَا إِلَىٰ أُمِّ مُوسَىٰ أَنْ أَرْضِعِيهِ

And We inspired the mother of Moses, saying: Suckle him

2. The way AB (اَبْ) is used for real father as well as grandfather, great father and ancestry, in the same way UMM (اُمّ) is also used. The way Hazrat Adam (Adam) is father of human race in the same way Hwwa (Eve) is the mother of human race.

3. As source or root. Like أُمُّ الْكِتَابِ (13:39) as Al-Lauh Al Mahfooz, أُمَّ الْقُرَىٰ (6:92). Means central village and metaphorically the city of Makkah (Mecca). Also UMM-UL-KHABAIS (اُمّ) (Mother of all evil) is alcohol, and UMM-UL-AMRAD (اُمّ) (Mother of ailments) is constipation. UMM-AT-TAREEQ (اُمّ) (Mother of ways) is the most traveled road, and UMM-UN-NUJOOM (اُمّ) (Mother of stars) is galaxy.

4. For respect and reverence. Allah says in the Quran

Suran Al-Ahzaab (The clans): سورۃ الأحزَاب 33:6

النَّبِيُّ أَوْلَىٰ بِالْمُؤْمِنِينَ مِنْ أَنفُسِهِمْ ۖ وَأَزْوَاجُهُ أُمَّهَاتُهُمْ

The Prophet is more worthy of the believers than themselves, and his wives are [in the position of] their mothers

5. As a patronymic. Sometimes there is connection in the patronym for instance UMM-UR-RUBAA’ WA-ARBAIEAN (ام اربع واربعین) which means a myriapoda, and while other times there is no connection in it for instance naming an infant girl UMME KULTHOOM (ام کلثوم) (Mother of kulthoom). But there is no such patronym in the Quran
